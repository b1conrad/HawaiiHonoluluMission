Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 40

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (March 22, 2026) morning, we woke up to the first day of the second half of our mission, marveling at the passage of time; how sometimes it seems slow and other times too fast. Even though our ward is having Fast Sunday this week because the next two Sundays are Easter and General Conference, we decided not to fast, due to Sara's diabetes (type two). So, she took her medication (which requires she not eat for at least half an hour (so we played a game of Skip Bo to wait)) and then we made a fruit smoothie and toast. Testimony meeting was great, and afterwards in Priesthood meeting, the teacher, brother Soon, drew a simple diagram.

It was a good and spirit-filled discussion, around the conference talk by Elder Andersen on the atonement, and how we can be forgiven. Jerry Staker visited again, and told us that he and his wife have a doctors appointment tomorrow, and will then put in their mission papers; how exciting. Dennis had given his testimony about the power of the Holy Ghost, in which he told how his mother-in-law (who passed away this morning) who spoke only Japanese lived in Virginia and always attended meetings, even though she couldn't understand the speakers. A few times, she did understand and always sought an autograph from such a speaker after the meeting, on the inside back cover of her Japanese Book of Mormon. The list includes several prophets and apostles. He left Bruce a copy of this and a write up of the story (see below).

In other news, a miniscule tender mercy: Bruce thought that he had left his tie clip in Waikiki and was sad because it is the only thing from his dad that he uses every day. He dreamt that it would reappear somehow and even envisioned it dropping from the sky onto the sidewalk. He found it — on the sidewalk. He hadn't left it behind; it had slipped out of his pocket yesterday.

The autographs she obtained from the men whom she could understand. [link to full report]

Monday morning, Bruce got up early enough to join a zoom meeting with Phil's students at 7am (next week it will be at 6am) and listening in on what they have done and what they hope to do was extremely interesting. He missed the last couple of meetings (due to the time change and early morning seminary security, so they had made a lot of progress (like when you haven't seen someone's kids for a few years — my how they've grown!)). The district council was good and we met the three new missionaries (one, Elder Churchill, brand new). Then we came home and Sara made a delicious lunch from prime rib leftovers. Brief nap and then drove to massage for Sara (administered by Kevin from our ward and his colleagues), while Bruce got a few books from the Kailua library around the corner. We drove straight home to rest. Dinner with the sisters at 6:30pm at Golden Pho, then home and in bed by 7:45pm.

Tuesday morning, Sara was up at 3am worrying that she might have hurt a sister's feelings. Bruce's alarm went off at 5am, and we both got ready and arrived at the stake center just minutes after the seminary teachers opened the gate, but before they unlocked the door. Sara practised the organ and piano in the chapel, while Bruce greeted the students, helped the teachers with screens, and generally watched over things (all was calm). After sunrise, he enjoyed standing outside in the beautiful weather. Sister Fetzer was planning on making it to the temple for an 8am session, but didn't leave until 6:59am; we assume she made it, and that made us a lot less worried about being able to arrive there at 8:30; we even had to go back to the apartment to get three personal cards for sealings, and still arrived at 8:04am, which was early enough that we were able to do those sealings and even more and still make the session.

Elder Walker, Bruce, Sister Krupp, Sara, Sister Naophiankham, Elders Frogley, Churchill, Miller. Missing Elders Kim (district leader) and Gilmore, who had a previously-arranged lesson.

We stopped at Keneke's in Punalu'u for lunch, and then napped at home until the sisters called and asked us to pick up a couple of dresses that they had ordered, in Aiea, which we did and then shopped at Costco in Waipio. Home to unload groceries, work puzzles, work on the blog.

Wednesday morning, Bruce woke up very early with a strange idea about social media; it was so compelling that he couldn't go back to sleep, and so he got up to write about it. In a nutshell, it is that the Family Search Shared Family Tree is in fact a kind of social media. So, he wrote up some memories of Merlin Litchfield so that a couple of stories would be rescued from the ravages of time and entropy. Then, Bruce got ready to go do early morning seminary security (wherein Sister Fetzer reported that she arrived at the temple at 7:36 yesterday). We arrived at the tabernacle in time, parking in the one handicapped spot (Bruce had been unduly worried about finding a parking spot). It was a very inspiring meeting and fun to see lots of friends from prior transfers. We were exhausted and rested the rest of the day. The sisters sent us a picture taken on Monday evening:

Sadly, we didn't get a picture of Sister Krupp's companion, Sister Naophiankham, photographer.

Thursday morning, we got up, had breakfast, and sat down in front of Bruce's laptop to attend the conference meeting in Maui over zoom, from 9am until noon. Otherwise, a day of rest and a bit of laundry. At 6pm, Bruce had Helaman drive him to the family search library so he could open the doors. Sara finished some cooking and came a bit later. We had no patrons. We are still thinking about some news that we got a couple of days ago letting us know that Tiffany's nephew Raymond died, and are trying to make sense of that. Sara spent some time with his mother, Stephanie, on the phone and sent her a card.

Friday morning, Sara got a phone call from a friend at 5:30am, and had a nice long talk with her; it is easy for people on the mainland to not think about the time difference. We will be getting ready to take Sara to her doctor as she has an appointment in Honolulu at 8:15am. It took about three hours, during which we talked with Doctor Lawler for a fruitful fifteen minutes. Sara has a torn meniscus and a small cyst on her ACL, both in the left knee. She'll be referred to a knee specialist. In the meantime, Sara is to strengthen the muscle on top of her thigh above the knee. We ate lunch at Betty's Burgers and got home just after noon and rested and did laundry. Shortly before four, Sara put the casserole into the oven, and we'll be leaving for the senior dinner in Mililani shortly, with a quick errand at Costco in Waipio on the way. We ate near the Lelegrens and that was nice because they will be leaving in ten weeks and this will probably be the last time we see them. A picture was taken.

In a couple of hours, at 9am, we'll have the last of three meetings with Elder Wada, this one also via zoom because he is meeting with the big island zones in Hilo today. Helaman drove to Sam's Club and purchased some rice and bottled water which he then delivered to a distribution place in Waialua for the assistance of those recovering from the recent flooding.

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2026

Sunday (July 5, 2026) morning, Bruce couldn't get back to sleep at 4:30am, so he got up to sit on the porch for sunrise. Looking first east and then south-southwest. [photos above fold!]

This view of Keahi a Kahoe shows its usual crown of clouds

We were prepared to help Tatiana with divorce papers, but the elders told us she wasn't going to come to church today. That allowed us some extra time. Bruce enjoyed sleeping in while Sara bathed. Our alarm awoke us in time to get ready for church. It was a wonderful fast and testimony meeting, and we learned that the Young family and the bishop's family did some crab catching together during the ward campout. Kamuela Kishida told of listening to the still small voice during a soccer game in which he had to goal tend against a penalty kick. This was followed by a Sunday School lesson in which we both participated more than usual.

We drove straight from church to Whole Foods, where—to our delight—they do sell broccoli sprouts, which Sara needs to help her sleep through the night. After eating there, we drove straight to a baptism service for Mikey, who has led an interesting life.

In upper right vignette: Elders Russell and Johns, Mikie, Sisters Bingham and Galligao

From the baptism, we drove straight home to pick up a casserole (that Helaman had baked for us); Bruce ran the broccoli sprouts up to the fridge, wrapped the casserole in a bath towel, and brought it downstairs with a pillow for insulation and then we drove to the senior potluck in Laie.

From the potluck we drove over to the Laie North Stake where we were treated to a fireside presented by Steven Sharp Nelson (of piano guys fame) and his family. It was wonderful.

Sara drove us home in the dark in less than an hour, uneventfully. Long day!

Monday morning we found out at 9:42 that district meeting is today this week, because the district has a temple P-day tomorrow. We rushed to get ready and arrived on time. It was a good meeting. Afterwards, we shopped at Times for a watermelon, some crab, and tomatoes; their prices are quite high, but they had the crab on sale (we had gotten a rain check the last time). Home for lunch, naps, and working on Sara's class. Helaman cooked a steak for us for dinner.

Tuesday, Sara is working on her class while Bruce naps.She made a delicious stew based on the leftover chicken broccoli casserole; that made a good lunch. Sara is making arrangements for Eli to stay near Reno for a singles conference there in a week or so. Bruce is preparing to pay our monthly missionary rent. At 5:01pm, we got a call from John Burgwinkel reminding us that they are taking us out to dinner; we thought it was to be tomorrow! We rushed over to Moonga's and joined them. After that, we checked the mail and the magazine containing our weekly puzzles was in the mailbox this week. Sara finished the sudoku about one minute ahead of Bruce; he, however, finished the crossword first. We are watching a movie, Crisis (2021), after Bruce finished his nightly work for the picos shadowing the temperature sensors we have back in Orangeville. Tomorrow will be a traveling day again.

Back to the story line, on Wednesday: we arrived early at the temple and were directed to go back outside and around to the back of the building, to the baptistry entry. We waited for a while and Bruce was asked to be the recorder for a group of confirmations. Shortly after ten, he went to the car (where we had left our phones) to text the elders who had made the appointment, and sure enough, the event had been canceled. So, we asked to be placed in with the group currently being baptized, and Bruce got to witness Sara being baptized for her cousin's first wife, Britten. Then, he did the confirmation. It was very moving. Later, this:

Helaman and Sara; Helaman and Elder Graff across the street where it was downhill

We left the temple, driving and thinking about where we might eat lunch. The PCC (our first choice) was closed, it being Wednesday. Sara was texting with Helaman who decided to join us at Keneke's, so that's what we did. We met Elder and Sister Graff, who are assigned in the PCC, and were also eating there. After eating, we found that Helaman needed to pushstart his motorcycle. As Sara described this experience, in a text to her children:

Exciting day! We went to the temple to be with a NEW member who did not show up. But I did a baptism and confirmation of David Sadler's deceased wife. It was very inspiring. Then we called Helaman and he agreed to have lunch with us at Keneke's hamburger place. He jumped on his Indian motorcycle and it was very very nice. Hamburger with goats and dogs and kittens. We left and Helaman couldn't get his motorcycle going. I pushed it (with much protestation from Helaman... I am stiff soaking in epsom salts right now) no start. Then another Elder from Oregon tried to help; both Helaman and he were EXHAUSTED! I went in and found my beloved Elder Kam. He gave it one push. Vroom vroom vroom, off Helaman went. Helaman, my baby, will sleep tonight...Bruce is already asleep . It's 3 pm!!!

Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 15

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025

Sunday (September 28, 2025) we got to church a little early, and eventually were joined by Elder and Sister Lowry, who had come to make presentations in the Elders' Quorum and Relief Society meetings. They gave us a cherry pie to thank us for having organized this. There were some very nice testimonies born, and tributes to President Nelson. We came home and napped after lunch. Bruce wants to share this picture of our front (and only) door taken July 5.

In the evening, we drove to choir practice; it is a very difficult arrangement of Joy to the World.

Monday shopping at Costco and filled the tank with just six gallons. The trip meter isn't reliable as it showed 770.6 when we left home but only 770.7 at the pump in Waipio (18 miles away)! Brought everything into the house and stuffed it all into the fridge and freezer. Naps. Figuring out the best way to invite people to watch general conference this coming weekend. Pasta with pesto, salad, canned corn, and grapes for dinner.

Tuesday, getting ready for the district council meeting and a dentist appointment for Sara at 11:50am. The district meeting was a true joy, very moving. We opened singing 'Til We Meet Again and Sara wept through it: only three out of eight of the young missionaries will be in our district next transfer. We wrote in the memory books of Sisters Jones and Anderson, and the former gave us a very touching thank you card.

The lesson by Elder Fowler on integrity/obedience was masterful and very spiritual, based on scriptures, Preach My Gospel, and a personal experience report from a transfer where he didn't understand the language, and yet was obedient and felt the spirit most strongly. Sara asked for a group picture afterwards. We shared the cherry pie with them.

Back: Jones, Pugh, Sara, Bruce, Hosaka, Fowler; front: Haycock, Anderson, Hirschi, McElroy

(home, staying, we are staying, Honolulu, other district; staying, Mililani, staying, other mission!)

We were on time to the dentist and it went well; we like Dr. Moon. He referred Sara to an endodontist (but who isn't covered by our insurance). We purchased lunch at Panda Express on the way home. Napped. Now preparing supper, and exchanged a purchase made earlier in the same day (senior discount day) at Ross Dress for Less.

Wednesday (October 1, 2025 (starting our fourth month)). Laundry.

Thursday, one of us watched the sunrise from our deck; another beautiful Hawaiian day. More laundry. In the evening, we attended a zoom meeting with the new missionaries in our zone, and it was as delightful to see the newcomers as the stalwart familiar faces. The three who have only been here two days really looked sleepy, as 9pm is early the next morning where they came from.

Friday we're worried about Sara's brother, Ross, who was hospitalized with a heart attack last night. We returned a bunch of aluminum cans and plastic bottles, for about three dollars, and went shopping at Safeway.

Saturday we were uplifted by three sessions of General Conference. This has been a difficult week, with much sorrowing over current events, both public and private.

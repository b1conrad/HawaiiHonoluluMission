Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 38

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (March 8, 2026) morning we enjoyed sleeping in for a bit. Then Sara took a bath while also taking a phone call from Eli. Bruce worked on this blog a bit, trying to figure out why the RSS feed doesn't seem to be working (unsuccessfully). We arrived at church on time, and liked the meeting, with three youth speakers and Brother Oh of the high council. At five pm we attended the baptism of Ike by his father Kela, and ate across from the Longs. We agreed to pray for their son Travis and they will pray for our son.

Monday morning, Bruce's alarm went off at 4am and he got to the church gate at 4:59. After checking the room behind every unlocked door in the building, he unlocked the northeast set of doors and sat down in a chair to wait watchfully. He greeted each person as they arrived with aloha and good morning. In an idle moment, he took a selfie of his reflection in the church door.

Selfie flipped to undo the reflection; south street garage

Our next destination was a parking garage in downtown Honolulu, where we arrived in the nick of time for a meeting that turned out really well. Back home, we dropped off a letter to one of the elders, visited the family search library with a question, then ate brunch at Pan's Kitchen, then came back to the apartment for naps and a celebratory game of SkipBo. Bruce sent off our monthly rent check. We heard from Mike Perry that our modem was replaced; sadly, this means that the Wovyn sensors are no longer working, so that hobby is on hold until we get back to Orangeville. Upon reflection, he realized that he just needs to open up port 3000 for the IP address of the new modem; so he wrote an email message to Mike asking about that and went to sleep.

Tuesday morning’s alarm was at 4:15 and Bruce got up quietly and left at 4:50am. The church gate was closed but not locked; besides that a work truck was idling at the curb, which was a bit scary. He opened the gate and drove in and parked; the truck followed him in! The driver got out and handed him a white box saying, “These pastries are for Sister Kia’s class.” Whew, problem solved. President Candari came in with a basketball and promised to lock up the gate, so Bruce was able to leave before the young men finished their play. Sara got up shortly after he got home and then we went to district council. The sisters gave a lesson on following the spirit so afterwards we took them to meet Onipa’a at the vitamin shoppe.

In the evening, we drove to the stake center and Sara played from a new booklet she received, of organ preludes, getting used to the organ. Then we went out into the foyer and sat on a couch and talked. From there we drove to pick up a food order at Jack in the Box and ran into Elders Kim and Nathan, who were going to come and pick up packages for Elder Fehoko, but didn't in the end.

Wednesday, we got up shortly after sunrise and drove to the Kualoa beach park, but the gates were still locked, so we drove just a little bit further, parked on the side of the road, and made our way over a few boulders into the sea. Elder Fehoko came by to get his packages. A few long talks with Helaman. Bruce texted Ashlyn a happy birthday text.

Thursday, we got up, ate breakfast, showered, and dressed, getting ready for the SYS meeting over zoom. In the evening we found the Chun's residence and gave them a salad and they gave us quite a few eggs because some of their family was leaving on a trip. Then, we drove to the stake center and opened up the gate and the family search library. During our time there, no patrons came in but we made some important improvements for Sara's uncle Kay, connecting him with aunt Lois, and choosing the correct parents for her. We were able to do this because Bruce found the marriage license and application for Kay and Lois. Her parents divorced shortly after her birth and her younger sister's; both parents remarried, and there are pictures of Lois with many of her step siblings and her step-mother. It was fun. Then we had a long talk with Helaman, spent some time with scripture study, and went to bed at 10:30pm.

Friday we got an early morning call from Eli because he's helping us help Tiffany to get housing. We arrived early for Sara's MRI and they took her in an hour early. There were very few people on the roads, as the Governor had asked everyone to stay home today. We stopped for a pastry at Liliha bakery; it was pretty good, sweeter than French pastries, but tasty. The power came on again as we walked in the door, but went out again just before sunset. Bruce took his shower just before it got too dark to see inside, and is now checking to see if he needs to do seminary security tomorrow morning; he will be doing that; setting an alarm and trying to sleep in the dark at 7pm. We watched a show first, so we fell asleep after ten.

Saturday early the power came back on, likely shortly after midnight, and the lights woke us up briefly. Bruce woke up spontaneously at 4:20am just before his alarm and got dressed and went to the stake center. Sara predicted no one would come. In the church was a persistent buzzing.

Alarm that kept sounding, cycling through these three displays

Having given up on silencing it, he took a chair outside the door, but still under an overhang and read a book while waiting for people to come. No one came, just as Sara had predicted. Bruce is not sure through what channel the word came that seminary had been canceled. Helaman cooked some steaks and set off our smoke alarm.

In the evening we went to the stake center for Legend's baptism at 6pm. A very strong spirit prevailed and we enjoyed conversing with one of his aunts at the meal afterwards.

Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 45

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (April 26, 2026) morning just before church, we received a picture of the second baptism yesterday afternoon, after which all MP holders were asked into the circle.

Elder Kam, mother, daughter who was baptized yesterday, Elder Quiner

We arrived at church early enough that the previous ward was still in session (though they must’ve run over time). The talks and lessons were great. After lunch and a nap, we headed back to the chapel for a baptism service at 6pm, both talks given by young men.

Elder Walker, Temu, Elder Miller

Monday is expected to be a big day, with car inspection at 8:30am and repairs to the apartment commencing at 9am: screen door removal, air conditioner repair/replacement, and oven replacement. Our oven has been acting up, automatically starting self-cleaning and/or locking the controls. First, we drove out to Helaman's truck parked on the street, but we didn't bring the right key, so drove back for it, then on our way to the garage where Bruce talks with Guy.

Back home to breakfast with the truck parked in a visitor spot because the motorcycle leaves insufficient room for it, although the car just fits. Before we could start cooking, the man came from maintenance to look at our issues, and removed the screen door hinges and latch while we ate. He will recommend a new A/C and a new oven/range. Naps followed. Guy called to say we wouldn’t pass until the left rear taillight was fixed. Bruce asked if he could do it. He didn’t know and we left it that he would call us in the afternoon one way or the other. But he hasn’t called at 5pm so Bruce is worried about getting the truck parked in the street. No one else seems to be worried about it. We got ready for bed, and Guy called about 7pm and the car was ready. We got dressed, and drove down in the pickup and Sara dropped Bruce off; he paid and drove home in the car. Long day; longer for Guy than it should've been.

Tuesday, slept in an hour longer than planned, having left the truck in a parking spot that, by observation, has always been empty, and had intended to move it before the yard workers started their day (but after the tow-away interval ended). Missed that window of opportunity. Got a warning while moving it to a good spot on the street. We also received an email message announcing a big change in our duties: we will no longer be preparing lunch meals for zone conferences, nor for the mission leaders on the interview day. Bruce renewed the car registration (for $292.25 (cash)). Home for breakfast, but first a phone call with Jessica, and preparing forms for the vehicle and apartment inspections this week. The district council was great, with an inspirational lesson from Elder Kim, who is leaving to Kauai, to be a zone leader for his last transfer or two. Sister Krupp is also leaving for one of the two districts in Kauai. Elder Miller is going to Mililani and Elder Washburn is going to the big island. We will surely miss this half of our district, while looking forward to meeting four new-to-us missionaries.

Sisters Krupp, Naophiankham; Elders Washburn, Frogley, Walker, Kim, Gilmore, Miller; us two

We picked up a lunch special at Safeway, and came home to eat; then Bruce drove to the elders apartment for an inspection while Sara spoke with Eli on the phone. On the way back, Bruce sold some empty cans and bottles for $3.15. At home, he scanned the inspection forms and this week's puzzles. We worked on the puzzles (Sara finished sudoku first), spoke with Daniel, and with Tiffany on the phone, read, and napped. Bruce forwarded two completed apartment inspection forms and three vehicle inspection forms (we handle the elders, and the Petersons and Larsons handle the rest of the zone). We had done the vehicle inspections after the picture, while the missionaries were involved in phone audits. It feels like it has been a long day, at 8pm.

Wednesday an early phone call broke through do-not-disturb before 6am on Bruce's phone and he can't figure out how that happened; it was disturbing to him. After breakfast, Bruce sent off a check for our Utah state tax payment for the 2025 tax year; we owed a lot (from a lump sum that allowed us to pay off our mortgage) because federal taxes had been withheld from retirement payments, but no state tax had been withheld. While Sara talked with Eli and Jessica, Bruce spoke with Elizabeth on the phone. After some talks with Helaman, we decided to spend a few days in Waikiki and made arrangements. We will leave tomorrow afternoon.

Thursday morning before 5am, another call came on Bruce's phone. How odd that the time limits he had set up have stopped working. Earlier, he had had a nightmare and fell/jumped out of bed, landing on his shoulder, and then went back to sleep. We finally found out where the rented apartment was, and left for the airport to pick up Alyssa; grabbed a burger at Mcdonalds; drove to the apartment and found it (810 not 801). It has an ocean view too tired to enjoy.

Friday morning, we enjoyed sleeping in. After listening to the president's speech live from the Villages (in Florida (thanks to the (taken for granted) miracle of planetary television)), we went to the beach. The tide was low, so we were all the way out to the breakwater wall, and had a good time taking in the water. We walked the two blocks back to shower, dress, and ate. Lazed about.

Saturday we awoke well-rested, and received news that three of our four young fruit trees have died, and the remaining one has fire blight. Mike will do what he can, which we appreciate. After watching a movie, we undressed and put on swimming suits and took in the ocean, which was very refreshing. Then we bought water bottles and sandwiches at an ABC store. Bathed and napping.

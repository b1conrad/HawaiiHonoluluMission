Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 36

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (February 22, 2026) morning we got to sleep in, then picked up Denise and Danny and drove to the tabernacle for the Manoa ward sacrament meeting. After the second hour, Bruce met a granddaughter of Ted Francis, his grandfather Art Conrad’s good friend and neighbor. Then we drove back to Waikiki and ate lunch at Teddy's bigger burger. Then the Spencers walked to the hotel, and we drove D&D to Safeway near their home. Unspoken (until now) consequences of these trips is that Bruce walked from the hotel to the parking spot at 11am, from the parking spot to the burger place, and four of us walked from the burger place to the parking spot, and Bruce walked back from the parking spot to the hotel. Lots of walking. Parking is not simple in Hawaii (shown in collage on next page)

Monday morning, after the meeting with Phil's students, we drove to the doctor's office, with the rationale that it wouldn't take any longer than wading through the phone voice response system. We had a good visit, and have begun scheduling an MRI and physical therapy, and got more prescriptions. Then, we drove to GNC to buy vitamin C and glutamine, then to Safeway where we purchased lunch and ate outside, having interesting conversations with locals. Then, we went back in and bought some groceries to get us through the next couple of days. When we got to the hotel, Sara went up with our purchases and Bruce drove over to Monserrat Avenue to find a parking spot, and walked back. He found one really close this time (the left green arrow below (other days the parking spots have been as far away as the right green arrow)). We stayed at this same hotel in March of 2025, parking each night on Monsarrat Avenue, so we had some experience with this. It's sometimes a very long walk, but saves $45 per night.

Same place last year; map arrows: orange→hotel, blue→beach, green→parking; today's spot

While it is a short walk to the beach that we like because of the protective walls, it is oftentimes a very long walk to the car. But not tonight! At sunset, Bruce went up to the roof of the hotel and watched the sun set. Usually, there is a bank of clouds at the horizon, but not tonight! He saw a very small green flash as the very top of the sun disappeared. Not since Mauritius 10 years ago!

Tuesday morning, we got up and walked to the beach to get the benefit of sea water; there was just a bit of gentle rain on the way back. Looking up at our hotel room from the street, onto the balcony from our room, and towards Diamond Head from the balcony and from the street.

We drove to Kaneohe for the district council meeting, and bought lunch at Safeway on Kapahulu Avenue (just off the freeway), and then watched the State of the Union speech. Afterwards, we went upstairs hoping to see the green flash, but there was a bank of clouds on the horizon, so no. But we did get a mist of rain and the corresponding rainbow (photos courtesy Sarah).

Wednesday we were awake at 2:30am wondering what to do because Sara could not sleep and wanted to go home to take some mucinex. We explored the possibilities: ABC stores were closed; Longs was open but quite a ways away; try again to sleep. In the end we went back to sleep for about four hours, then got up and walked to the beach where we took in the sea and early sunshine. From there, we walked directly to the car. A strange man on the sidewalk "shot" at us with a finger gun and beatboxing as we passed him on the sidewalk; the lady who was setting up an art display/sale along the sidewalk gave him the finger; Sara smiled at him disarmingly. After talking to her, the lady agreed to take the canvases that we had removed weeks ago from an elders apartment and had been carrying around everywhere in our car, so Bruce walked them back to her. We returned to Kaneohe without incident. Meanwhile, Helaman was at the doctors office and so Bruce parked the Scion in a good spot on the street, and then met him there to move the Scion out of the way so he could park his truck there.

We talked, ate, napped, did laundry, etc. and then the day ended with us back in our usual digs. Helaman saw a man (who was moving out) throw a KitchenAid machine into the Dumpster, so Bruce went out and retrieved it; Helaman cleaned it and it seems to work just fine. So that was fun.

Thursday we got a call from Aunt Janis and so got up early and ate breakfast. Bruce received an email message about the deposit of the next Canadian dollar check, so he paid our tithing for February. A set of elders wanted Books of Mormon, so Bruce drove eight copies to their house and got back in time for the 10am weekly zoom meeting. Continuing with laundry. Jasmin came and power-washed our porch, and Bruce scrubbed some of it harder with a scrubbing brush and some ammonia; it is nice and clean now. We went out to pick up something for Heaman, then dropped it off and Bruce got the church keys and we went to run the family history center, where all of the youth came to print off names for baptisms in the temple next month. It was quite the flurry of activity.

Friday we got up and Sara made a fruit drink from many things we have in the freezer, from grapes to apples to blueberries, etc. We played a game of SkipBo, then bathed and dressed. Bruce worked on the discussion he has been asked to lead next Thursday evening. Sara is preparing for shopping so that she can make a to-go salad for our mission leaders who will be interviewing the missionaries in our zone on Fast Sunday. We drove to Kailua to pick up an order of food but it got cancelled. Bruce exchanged some books at the library and Sara picked up a pizza at Whole Foods and we drove home to eat and rest. Bruce finished the sudoku puzzle first, surprisingly, and also the crossword puzzle. Sara stayed up late talking with Helaman. Much of the food we had ordered ended up being delivered late after dark.

Saturday we did a little shopping. Sara has a horrible cold. The end of this week is also the end of this month.

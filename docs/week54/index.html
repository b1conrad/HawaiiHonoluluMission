Hawaii Laie Mission — Week 54

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2026

Sunday (June 28, 2026) morning we cleaned the filters of our bedroom air conditioner and the blades of the overhead fan. Haircut. When we got to church, Bruce was recruited to figure out how to get the slide show in the foyer to auto play (after starting the presentation, hover over the bottom left corner and click on the three dots, then on the autoplay option and select loop and how often, and finally hit play). It was nerve wracking, but that was because he was looking up at the big screen while fiddling with the track pad; the secret is to look at the small screen—he knew that from past experiences, but the clock was ticking. As it was, he got it going just as the meeting was about to start, and slipped into the pews just before the new bishop stood up.

The first speakers were two people that we had greeted during a sacrament meeting many months earlier; the daughter was later baptized January 31st (see our Baptism bookends post) so this was her first talk, ever. Her father, who is self-reactivating, was the second speaker. Then came our Elders Quorum president, one verse of an intermediate hymn, and our out-going bishop's wife and finally former bishop Vuikadavu. It was a sweet meeting. In the second hour, Bruce participated more than he usually does, and met Eric Young, with whom he talked a lot as they have a lot in common; they exchanged phone numbers and text messages.

In the evening, Sara was pleased to see that she now has access to her course on Canvas through BYU Pathways. The class is Finance 300 and is the last one she needs for her degree from BYU [Provo] because it will mean she has satisfied all of the requirements for a BGS (Bachelor of General Studies) degree with an emphasis in business management, and then all she has left is a two credit capstone and she will have earned the degree. We fiddled with it for  a while and then went to sleep.

Monday morning we had scripture study, prayed, prepared and ate breakfast. Then she cleaned off the desk in our room and sat down to go to work. Bruce tried paying her tuition, and saw that she had earned a 10% discount by working through an activity. Tuition is still not payable though. We had a brief glitch when the text encouraged her to share her experience on WhatsApp and we had a brief flurry of activity trying to get access to that. We cannot unless she is willing to connect her smartphone to the Internet, which she is not. We decided it was an optional activity and she went on. There was another brief problem as the audio got out of sync with where she was reading the coursework; we solved it by stopping the audio and restarting it.

She got a lot done in the class, including studying several sections of the text and taking three quizzes. Then she started working on a pastry which is to be given to Elder Walker tomorrow, since we drew his name in a gift exchange at the previous district council meeting. The crust is made from crumbled cookies from the emergency rations that we've been saving for a while; fruit will be added tomorrow to finish it off. We ended the day watching the movie The Bonfire of the Vanities (1990). [A first: no image above the fold this week]

We received an email message from Sister Haycock, including some pictures.

Sister Haycock on the big island (with cows in the background) posing with a Book of Mormon

Tuesday morning, we got up and ready for district council. That involved finalizing the treat for Elder Walker — that we started yesterday — by cutting up fruit and adding it into the shell. The meeting was uplifting and it was fun to have everyone share the treat they had brought. Bruce began to be uncomfortable as he thought of all the sweet treats, and what would we be given? He needn't have worried because Sister Martin brought out a vegetable tray for us! Everything else had been rich brownies, cookies, cakes, etc. which Sara would not be able to eat.Bruce drove back to the church right away because when we got home there was yet another package waiting for Elder Walker, and he got there just as they were leaving; it was a good thing because the package we had delivered to him from yesterday he had hidden under the podium on the desk and had forgotten to take it, so Bruce went in and got that one for him too. On the way home, he stopped at the library (not on-the-way per se) to pick up some requested books that had arrived. We both napped and Sara bathed. It is so hot in the apartment that we decided to move to the chapel (which is kept very cool (to avoid a mold problem)) and Sara began work on her class assignments while Bruce added to this blog post. Puzzles not in mail this week.

Tuesday is also exactly one year to the day from our arrival at the Honolulu airport (see our Arrival post)!

Wednesday dawned, and with it the month of July. We met  two elders in our ward for dinner at about 4:30 pm at Chili Rellenos nearby. Sara got a lot done in her class; Bruce read.

Thursday we have the mission-wide zoom meeting. And a session for family history at 6pm. Sara completed a quiz while there and Bruce warmed up some food in the microwave. Bruce found the puzzles online and printed them. Sara finished sudoku first with seconds to spare, while Bruce was first on the crossword puzzle. Apparently, our subscription has terminated, so Bruce signed up online for three years (the shortest term on offer), so that we'll have puzzles for the duration of our mission. And whoever inherits our apartment will also be receiving it.

Friday we went with the elders to visit Tatiana. Then we shopped at Safeway.

Saturday is the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence.

About to be relieved after our shift managing this inflatable at the Polynesian Cultural Center

We left our apartment at 2:23pm and drove towards Laie for our shift at the free event. Along the way we stopped in Punaluu to eat an early dinner at Keneke's Grill. There we met a family from Layton, Utah, and had a fun visit with them while waiting for our order.

We didn't know where gate 10 was, so Bruce dropped Sara off at the obvious place and drove around looking for the gate. Finally found it on the BYUH campus and parked where he knew he could and then walked about half a mile until he found the gate. There were parking spots closer to the gate, but there was no point in going back and advancing the car. We were late for our shift and faked it, taking care of making sure there weren't too many kids inside while keeping it flowing. The Andersons arrived ten minutes early for their shift and we had them take this picture of us before we handed over our event/staff lanyards. When we were about ten minutes from Kaneohe, Sara ordered some food from Jack in the Box and we picked it up on the way home. In bed by 7pm.

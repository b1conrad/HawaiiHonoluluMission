Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 33

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (February 1, 2026) we enjoyed sleeping in. We got to church in plenty of time and met some new friends there and experienced a wonderful set of testimonies, after the meeting got underway with a baby blessing and the confirmation of Aaliyah, with so many of her extended family there to support her. We left before the second meeting to take Denise and her son to the doctors, and it ended up somewhat tragically, because she lost her smart phone somewhere and that is devastating. Sometimes you try to do something nice to help someone and then it doesn't turn out. We are so sad for her but don't know how we could make it better. In the end, she was able to locate it! An answer to our prayers. One of the zone leaders came by with a package for us to give to the mission president tomorrow.

Sara gave her a postcard of the temple, as we had been challenged to do in district council.

Monday morning is really windy, and one of a cluster of four palm trees actually blew over overnight. Bruce attended a meeting with Phil Windley's capstone class. By the time he went outside the fallen tree had been removed by landscape staff.

Elder Frogley (and his companion-of-the-day, Elder Hilson (from the south district)) were taking a trip to the mission office, so Bruce gave them the package for the president.

We went home to get food for Sara (who does not eat pizza (because of not eating wheat)), and then took a trip to the Kailua library, dropping off cans and bottles on the way ($4). Bruce got some additional books to read and we returned home.

Wednesday, we awoke to a rather cold morning (64°F at sunrise) and one of us spent some time near the oven in the kitchen to warm up. Bruce worked on this document and read, while Sara slept to make up for some late night conversations with her children. Laundry day. We got a phone call from Elder Hollingsworth giving Sara a contact person to call tomorrow at 8:30am. Bruce lost badly on the weekly puzzles, not even finishing the Sudoku and getting -32 on the crossword puzzle compared to Sara's -11. Sara is the winner!

Thursday morning, Sara was able to talk with someone at Ensign College about an equivalent course for her degree; we are hopeful. During that, Bruce got a call from Lethbridge about his missing Canadian dollar retirement check, which they will ask to be re-issued. We went to a movie and then picked up some food at Jack in the Box. We went to the stake center for the ward study class and to host the family history center. Came home and Bruce attended the evening transfer zoom meeting, while Sara retired for the night with a very sore knee.

Friday we got a phone call from Ben telling us that the county is taking out the trees south of the Price house, claiming that they are not on the lot, but on the right-of-way. Breakfast and SkipBo game. Trying to access a hearing for Tiffany; we learned that her parole hearing yesterday was a success and apparently she will be released in a month. Shopping at Safeway.

Saturday we enjoyed another cool morning, and rainy day. We had a visit from the sister missionaries, and saw a set of elders at our ward's movie night.

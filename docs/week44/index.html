Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 44

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (April 19, 2026) Bruce was happy to receive a haircut from Sara out on the front porch in the beautiful sunshine. After breakfast we got ready for church, which was wonderful, with great speakers and a lively Sunday school discussion.

After a quick lunch, we drove to the Keolu chapel, arriving at 3:02 for the baptism of Iliema Lolohea (of the Tongan branch). When we arrived, they were taking the before picture.

Elder Berry, Iliema, Elder Tupola

For the baptism itself, some sisters suggested that her long hair be tucked into the back of the white jumpsuit she was wearing, and that was done just before Elder Berry successfully immersed her on the first try. Bruce was invited to join the confirmation circle; always a privilege. The four elders and the branch president were the only ones invited into the circle, which was a bit unusual for there to be so few men.

Monday it turns out that Sara hadn't slept most of the night, worried about her grandson who is being drugged by his mother in an attempt to control his behavior. We are really worried about him and the situation. Bruce asked the Petersons if they had the other picture taken; they did!

Elder Tupola, Elder and Sister Peterson, Iliema, President Sulunga , Sara, Bruce, Elder Berry

We were relieved to learn during the day that the mother decided not to continue the medication until a mediation ordered it, so that the immediate danger has been at least postponed. Sara is reading the Bible in a week, along with many other people in the (U.S. of) America.

Tuesday we attended district council meeting where Elders Miller and Walker taught a lesson on unity. We drove home via Safeway to buy lunch. While Sara continued her Bible-reading project, Bruce drove on some errands: an appointment with Guy to get our car safety inspection, dropping off a can of pumpkin pie filling to the Chuns; getting some books at the library.

Around three-thirty, we decided to drive to Costco. On the way, Sister Edwards, the mission nurse, phoned us and we learned that Elder Kim had fallen and hurt his knee. We needed to go buy him crutches and painkillers. So, after meeting Helaman at Costco and completing our purchases there, we drove back to Kaneohe to put away the food and then to Longs Drugs to purchase crutches, and finally up to Waikane to deliver them and visit with Elder Kim and his companion-on-exchanges Elder Frogley. Sara drove home because it was around sunset and she does any night driving. The sisters came to pick up a package that had arrived earlier in the day and we were asleep by ten.

Wednesday, we got up expecting the SYS zoom meeting which had been scheduled for today instead of the usual Thursday, but got a messenger message saying it will be tomorrow after all. As the day wore on, and the temperature outside in the sun exceeded 80°, the inside temperature was well over 90° and Bruce wonders how it gets so hot inside; there are drapes covering all the windows, keeping out the sunlight. After surviving the heat, it was nice to watch a movie together, A Piano for Mrs. Cimino, and then fall asleep.

Thursday, we slept in just a little bit. After breakfast and a game of Skip Bo, we were edified by the weekly SYS meeting over zoom. Lunch was put together from miscellaneous leftovers in the fridge. Later in the afternoon, Sara built a couple of shepherds pie dishes, and we got them in the oven just in time to leave for the ward scripture study, on the tabernacle, complete with this scaled down reconstruction.

The inner court, with Sara and several ward members

We got home just before 9pm and watched a movie, The Next Three Days.

Friday morning: Elizabeth's 39th birthday. We went shopping at Safeway, then picked up the drinks we had been refrigerating in the chapel and drove to Honolulu for the senior dinner. It was held in the party room of the Banyan Tree Plaza apartments, where several mission couples live. Just outside the open window, at sunset, a multitude of noisy birds come home to roost. After eating, we were divided into teams of four — breaking up couples — for a game.

Clockwise: Pair of parakeets; Rose-ringed parakeet; Party hats worn by teammates Bruce and Sister Mangum; Everyone; Some of the teams in game led by Elder Hollingsworth (lines added)

Sara drove us home and we were in bed shortly after nine pm.

Saturday we attended a video call so that Ammon and Elizabeth could share their respective slide presentations of their favorite names for their child due this Fall. Sara wasn't up to going out, as she is involved in reading the Bible along with many in the United States of America this week, but Bruce went to a couple of baptisms. First, Reign, baptized by his older brother Azien, after a talk by his cousin Syah (the latter two themselves recently baptized (see earlier weeks)).

Syah, Brother Tani of bishopric, Elder Kam, Reign, Azien, little brother in front, Elder Quiner

We haven't yet received a picture from the second baptism service, for Sanalei, which was conducted a couple of hours later. In bed late, after transfer news (to be discussed next week).

Hawaii Laie Mission — Week 53

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2026

Sunday (June 21, 2026), the summer solstice and first day of astronomical summer has arrived. We got up, bathed, and ate breakfast. Eli phoned, and then Jessica. Myrna and Arlene sent text messages wishing Bruce (and their brothers-in-law) a Happy Father's Day. Later, more such came from Emily, an unknown texter, Carlie, Ashlyn, and Elizabeth. During the sacrament meeting, the ward got a new bishopric: Bishop Kamuela Kimokeo, with councilors Willy Frost and Malu States. The latter is new in the ward; he and his wife were the speakers last week.

Showing the sunny nature of today: Keahi a Kahoe in full sun

Before church, Sara had worked hard to prepare some lamb stew meat for the slow cooker, and we left it on low while we were gone. Later in the afternoon, we found it quite tasty, so it was a success validating the work. Sara spoke with Eli and Bruce with Elizabeth, during which latter call, two of Eli's boys wished Bruce a Happy Father's Day. We got a call from Tiffany and paid her phone bill; she has been having trouble getting work—even DI would not hire her. Around sunset we sat together on the porch and discussed a problem with someone who is quietly bad-mouthing us to stake and maybe mission leaders. Very discouraging; need to know who.

Monday we dropped some clothing off at the truck that accepts used things (Goodwill?), then drove back home to pick up Helaman. We drove into Honolulu for an important meeting, arriving quite early. So, Bruce had time to walk the box containing our former headboard to be returned via UPS. It was a six minute walk there, because he didn't know where he was going, but only four minutes back to the meeting place. The next important meeting is set for November 16.

Back home and we are so hot because we have been asked to turn off the AC when we are gone, to reduce the power bill. But that doesn't work well; it takes hours before we can cool the place below 90 degrees. We are discouraged, as we would be willing to pay the bill so we can be comfortable. We wrote an email message to that effect, so we are waiting to see what will happen. We watched a movie called The Borrowers.

Over ninety degrees, butter melts; high measured 97°; lowest ever was 76°

Tuesday dawned. We dropped off some used bottles and cans and a couple of knickknacks that we no longer wanted. All on the way to district council meeting, which was uplifting. Pictures were taken. Trying to locate one that includes us, but here is one taken by Sister Martin.

Sister Martin was holding a duck, and Bruce a basketball, props used (in a lesson on first contacts (taught by Sisters Tapola and Tupou)) in role plays. Below, us and the Martins.

After naps, Bruce drove some copies of the Book of Mormon to the chapel for the elders.

The picture Bruce sent them, inviting them to zoom in, to see where he set down the package

Sara is preparing some food: deviled eggs and chicken broccoli casserole, with Bruce serving as her sous chef. While waiting for things to cook, we are watching the movie Diamond Head, from 1959, starring Charleton Heston. We kind of lost interest. When we did the puzzles, Bruce finished both first, the sudoku just by a few seconds, though. Finished the movie.

Wednesday, we got up and did a little house cleaning. Bruce ran some errands: dropping off some more things we don't need at Goodwill, dropping off books at the library, picking up books at the library, and finally, getting the oil changed at Jiffy Lube. Watching a movie, The Hireling. The chicken broccoli casserole is a great success, eaten along with asparagus, finishing with strawberry shortcake (actually angel food) with ice cream. Reading and resting and planning for the long day tomorrow. We have asked for more pictures from yesterday and are waiting for those to arrive (they did, and were inserted in the page above). A couple of Skip Bo games; we are pretty even. Sara fell asleep while we were watching David and Goliath (1960).

Thursday, we were up before the crack of dawn and Bruce wrote up his report about his work at BYU Hawaii; Sara had slept a full six hours which is a new and welcome thing for her. We had a quick bite and headed over to Mililani for Zone Conference.

Bruce's not-very-good picture of the picture-taking just moments too late

It was a very important conference for us. We learned that senior couples, while allowed to attend (and have lunch), are not required to attend. Nevertheless, we found it very inspiring. During a break, Bruce asked the mission president point-blank if he was involved in the bad mouthing we are suffering with, and learned that no, he is not, and that we can trust him.

We also met Sister Jensen, the mission secretary (and photographer caught above), and her husband, Elder Jensen, who is the vehicle coordinator. The latter explained to Bruce how they do vehicle inspections in this mission—we will be helping at the next zone conference in Kaneohe in about six weeks. The mission leaders gave a presentation about the difference between cows and buffalo reacting to a winter storm: the cows turn their backs on the storm and try to run away, thus making it last longer for them; buffalo put their heads down and walk into the storm and thus get through it faster. Bruce was inspired by that to be direct with the President.

Then the young mission leaders (assistants to the president and sister leaders) gave a delightful presentation about "the green brick road" with Dorothy being a new friend progressing towards baptism. The tin man a member, the scarecrow the bishop, and the lion the elders quorum president and relief society president. Oh, the munchkins, the missionaries. They also spoke of the attack of the flying monkeys, repelled by the team of all the others. It was fun, and with a few brief selections from the movie to punctuate it all.

A lunch was served by the Mililani Stake. Afterwards, Sister Tupou gathered the seniors together, lined us up against the wall and shot our picture. She uses a standalone camera.

Bruce, Sara, Sister and Elder Morris, Sister and Elder Martin, Sister and Elder Bennett

We are by far the senior seniors in this picture, having been missionaries for a year—more than twice as long as the others combined. In the evening we took our shift at FamilySearch.

Friday morning we got up and made breakfast. Then, Bruce went back to bed because he stayed up late reading, while Sara worked a sudoku puzzle (not the weekly 9x9, but 16x16 of which she has a whole bookful). The sisters called and asked us to open the stake center for them so they could print out some things for an activity at the watch party tonight. While there, we spoke with Elder and Sister Kaonohe in the family search room. A contractor, Josh, came by and asked if we could open the gate for him tomorrow. Bruce went out and tried his key in the other gate and it works there; we exchanged numbers. We shopped at Safeway, because Friday specials. Helaman called us while we were there, but we were already checking out, so he went later by himself. We read and rested. Bruce awoke just in time for us to leave for the watch party at the stake center. We hadn't gone very far before sisters called us and asked us to bring some friends for them, so we did that, and brought Steven, James, and Ben.

The setup, photographed in the morning, and again in the evening with all the people

We left just before dark, Sara having created a large plate of food for our new friends to enjoy at home. We dropped them off, and then because it was dark, Sara drove home.

Bruce received from Michael a link to the video of the Sunday sacrament meeting and selected images from the speeches of the new bishopric, and put them together in this collage:

It was a free community event, the Tabernacle Choir at Temple Square Hollywood Bowl watch party. There was lots of food and several activities outdoors, and the broadcast from Hollywood inside in the gymnasium. It was well-attended, with lots of visitors, so also a missionary activity.

Saturday morning, Bruce left early to unlock the west gates at 7:30. He invited Josh and Rob to attend church, in a natural manner and they thanked him. He showed Josh (the contractor who is replacing a lamppost knocked down when a tree fell over in the winds last year) where to get water for setting the concrete of the base. Once that cures, an electrician will come to set up the post itself and wire in the lamp. Upon his return, Sara had breakfast ready. Now we are resting.

Sara is calling around to find a supplier of broccoli seeds so that we can grow our own sprouts; she finds these helpful in reducing her blood sugar level, along with the other things we purchased on Monday (which we failed to mention above (we had other things on our mind)) when we stopped at the Down to Earth Organic & Natural store. Bruce knew where it was because it was in the same shopping center as UPS where he had dropped off a package. He stopped in a space that had room for our car but wasn't a parking space and waited in the car while Sara and Helaman walked into the store. After that we drove to Costco to fill up with gas and groceries. End of flashback to Monday.

Elizabeth told us about a livestream for her BYUI Choral Alumni concert, so we tuned in to that, and found her. Her choir was intermixed with other musical groups. First with the band.

If the elevator had worked, we would have dropped it at the mission office on the fourth floor. Back in Kaneohe, we purchased some hair spray at Longs. Naps ensued.

He notes with interest that each of the dozen screenshots were bigger than five megabytes. 5Mb was the size of the hard drive that he had for the four years he was an assistant professor at the University of Lethbridge.

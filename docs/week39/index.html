Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 39

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (March 15, 2026) morning (the Ides of March) we got up and had breakfast and a talk with Helaman while planning activities for Sara's birthday later this week. We went to church, where several members of the Matavale family spoke and sang, because Elena, the youngest daughter, has finished her mission in Mexico. Then Sunday School taught by Scott, in which Sara shared a personal story. Afterwards we went to a brief choir practice.

Monday two elders came to wash their car. Bruce finished his books and napped, and talked with Helaman. Sara mostly slept away a headache. The big winds blew away the dove nest above our screen door, sometime in the last few days.

Tuesday morning, we learned that Craig Livingston, a dear friend of ours, died earlier this month. Sara had been trying to reach him, and finally obtained his sister's number and phoned her this morning. We had had no inkling that he might not last out the time of our mission. After district council, we inspected three cars and one apartment. Oh, but first, this photograph:

Elders Kim, Walker, Quiner (S), Miller, Frogley, Kam (S), Fehoko (Maui), Niueliku (Maui); Sisters Boehme (home), Krupp, Sara and Bruce.

Two of the elders will stay in our zone, but move to the south district (S); two elders are going to Maui, and Sister Boehme is going home. So, we're losing half of them, and will get three more.

We went to a baptism service at 5pm. Sara was asked to play the piano for both hymns and Bruce led the singing for the closing hymn, after Sara realized that that would be necessary.

Elder Fehoko (who baptized), Ezra, Elder Frogley (who confirmed)

Wednesday we woke up to Sara's birthday. Yay! After breakfast, we drove to the elders apartment in Waikane to do the formal inspection, then dropped off cans and bottles. A game of Skip Bo ensued, then naps all around. We went to the satellite city hall to purchase a senior bus card, but needed an appointment and it costs quite a bit annually (but the fare is then only $1.25 instead of $3 (and we won't be taking the bus often enough for it to pay for itself)). We packed for a three day staycation in Waikiki and Helaman drove us there, and then after we unloaded, to the Chart House where were just a bit too late for the happy hour, but we sat outside until the musical program started and were able to listen to a few numbers by Kamuela Kimokeo (of our ward bishopric) until Helaman came back to pick us up and drive us to our room.

We found one chair across from the Chart House and shared it for half an hour

Thursday morning we enjoyed sleeping in, Skip Bo, and breakfast. Tiffany called with some problems, from the cell phone of a woman who was helping her. We had calls with Mike, who was able to find her ID in a box we had packed away before leaving, and calls to Neva and Ryan and again the helpful woman, and then Tiffany's sister. In between, we walked two blocks to the beach and spent a few minutes in the water; very nice. Shortly after we got back to the apartment it began raining heavily. In the evening we went to an all you can eat restaurant nearby, the Hyatt Regency Waikiki Beach Resort and Spa.

Friday morning, we were awake early (like 3am), worried about Sara's purse which we hadn't seen recently. Helaman helped by looking in the truck and car but it wasn't in either of them. We did a more thorough search and found it in Bruce's computer bag, a place where we have never put it in the past, and neither of us remember doing it; we're losing it we guess. It has been raining all night; our swimsuits aren't dry yet, and the rain is expected all day. Nevertheless, we walked to the beach at about 2:30, with no rain, and took in the water.

Selfies before and after [inset] being in the water on Friday

Later, we walked back as far as McDonalds and tried their new burger, and went to bed early.

Saturday morning, we woke up to a pretty solid rain. We decided to fix breakfast from most of the food we had left in the refrigerator, which we did, and then started packing up, because we need to check out before 10am. After we had everything in order and placed by the door, we walked down to the beach one last time, since not only had the rain stopped, but the sun had come out from behind the clouds. We got back and waited until the alarm Bruce had set went off, giving us enough time to get everything down the elevator, and for Bruce to put the key fob back into the lockbox near the security desk.

Closeups of the two balconies from the corner room we stayed in; fridge on the balcony!

Having enjoyed some time off in the Kuhio Village Tower 2, we decided to end with a bang at Haleiwa Joe's this evening, in Kaneohe.

This week concludes the first half of our mission.

Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 41

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (March 29, 2026) dawned cooler than it has been; very nice. We got to church at 11am for choir practice and then the Easter program was great. All seven men of the bishopric (including the clerks and secretary) spoke, interleaved with the ward choir number and a song by the primary. And yet, the meeting finished on time! Kamuela, conducting, at the start called it an aggressive program, but it went really well.

Monday morning, the alarm Bruce set for 5:30 also went well, and he was able to join Phil's zoom meeting at 6am after showering and dressing. The students ran through their demo script and it is pretty great. He has still been unable to fix our blog's RSS feed problem, and also spent some time looking into a pico engine bug, at Phil's suggestion. We were sleepy and enjoyed a nap mid-day. Helaman drove to pick up a friend, Brent, who has come to visit him, at the airport. In the evening, we finally received the picture taken before our zone conference started last Wednesday, which consisted of all the missionaries on Oahu along with the zone leaders and sister training leaders from other islands.

We're in the very back; Sara is the pale blue dot on the aisle of right side, Bruce beside her

Tuesday morning, we got up early, and drove to the family search library, where Sara began an Enos day. Bruce attended the district council meeting, wherein Elder Frogley led a fruitful discussion on teaching the apostasy. Bruce managed to fix the stuck RSS feed (see repo). He checked the mail and printed out the two puzzles we do each week. Later in the night, while working in Family Search, Bruce discovered that his French ex mother-in-law died over a year ago (at age 98), which saddened him just a little bit — he had mistakenly thought it would never be too late to write her another letter.

Wednesday morning we awoke to text messages from Daniel, and from Myrna, and phone calls with Tiffany; the latter mostly about funeral services for her nephew. We ate breakfast with Helaman and Brent, and then they went on a fishing trip. Sara took a bath, while Bruce works on genealogy and blogs and a book he is reading, 38 Londres Street.

Thursday got up and checked to see if there was still an advisory about going into the ocean, but didn't get a clear answer. After breakfast, Bruce repotted his plant and we had a long conversation with Helaman and Brent. Then we attended our SYS meeting over zoom. In the evening, Bruce went in for our shift in the family search library, but there were no patrons.

Friday we went shopping at Costco, partly to prepare for lunch next Tuesday for the president's interview day. Bruce called Elder Chun, so that we could invite him and his wife; they will come and bring corn and rice. We went to the chapel to help clean, at 7pm and did a small shopping trip to Safeway on the way home. Watched a show, A Song To Remember.

Saturday, the alarm went off at 5:30am and we dressed and watched the first hour of the conference, then drove to pick up Denise, listening along the way. We arrived at the Kaneohe Stake Center just after 8am and began preparing breakfast, as Elder Kim had wanted. He and his companion arrived shortly thereafter and then the rest of the district and we ate breakfast together and listened to the afternoon (10am here) session. By 12:30pm we had cleaned up and were driving Denise home again. She really enjoyed it and we were glad Sara had been inspired to invite her. The sisters gave us a card with some reimbursement for the dresses we purchased on their behalf last week.

We arrived home to find that Helaman had purchased a motorcycle, so our parking spot is shorter than usual. Long talk with him about it and other things.

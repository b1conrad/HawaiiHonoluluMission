Hawaii Laie Mission — Week 56

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2026

Sunday (July 12, 2026) morning, Bruce woke up at 6:27, waiting to hear from the elders whether we need to take Tatiana to her ward at 8am. He had been up late working on an alternate way to read this blog (last commit was at 2:07 am). In the end, Tatiana got a ride, so we were able to rest until time for our church meeting. Elder Garner spoke first, and then Elder Gilmore. In the priesthood meeting, we each introduced ourselves and spoke briefly about a favorite prophet or two. We got home in time to sign into a zoom meeting with Elder Rasband, whose family is on vacation here. He blessed us that we would know that our calling is divine. Baptism at 3pm.

Elder Walker baptized Serenity, peeking out beside him; Bentley baptized by Elder McElroy

Monday we slept in a bit. Bruce completed his experiment for reading the archived blog posts (final commit). Working on Sara's class. Phone call from Tiffany.

Tuesday morning, Bruce had a meeting with Phil Windley at 5:10am over zoom, and he found it very interesting. Sara had a Teams meeting with a tutor at 7am, and it took about ten minutes to get logged in, but it was a very useful session. After it, Sara re-took the quiz and got 100%! We are now preparing to attend the weekly district council meeting. It went well, with a great testimony from the Martins and an inspiring training by the Centerville sisters, including some interesting role play sessions. After the meeting, Bruce locked up the chapel and one of the gates (never knowing why both had been opened). Then we went to Safeway and bought lunch.

The day ended with a thirty minute zoom meeting with the mission housing coordinator, which we watched while enjoying delicious tacos that Sara had prepared while Bruce napped.

Wednesday morning, Bruce got up to the sunrise, and mountain views while reading. Phone call from Destinn about the dinner he is hosting on Friday. Bruce was happy to learn that Nicole is going to be baptized on Sunday. We are asked to bring salad and pineapple.

Clouds hiding the highest point of Keahi a Kahoe, which is gradually revealed

Brief visit from elders returning some glass bowls. Sara worked on her class, with technical help from Bruce as needed. He sold some empty cans and bottles ($2.30) and got some new books at the library. We ate crab for lunch, and various leftovers and fruit for dinner. We fell asleep to the movie Crimson Tide (1995).

Thursday morning, Bruce woke up at about six and sat outside for a while to take in the view and sunshine. It’s 75° which is really nice. Currently it’s 80° in Orangeville (but it’s a dry heat), where four hours ago (at the same time of day (6:40)) it was 74°. Attending the zoom meeting called Iron Rod. Presentation on defending personal boundaries. Naps ensued. The sisters called, needing copies of the Book of Mormon; Bruce met them at our car, because we carry a box with us at all times. Getting ready to cover our shift in the family history library. Bruce sent an email message to Phil Windley and heard back from him before the day was over. More naps and then we went to the stake center to be available for questions in the family search room. We were able to answer several questions. Home after 8pm to eat supper and go to bed. We enjoyed drifting off into sleep.

Friday morning, Bruce was awake first, sitting on the porch, taking in the view and the 76° air; no wind at all, which is unusual here on the windward side of the island. Orangeville was 69° four hours ago (so the same clock time as of this writing). Today we intend to work on Sara's class, shop the Friday specials at Safeway, and prepare for and attend the evening dinner hosted by Destinn at his home. Speaking of home, ours from a couple of angles:

Our apartment is in the southeast corner of building 14, on the upper floor (from the corner to the downspout). Our bedroom window is between the downspout and the balcony. There are seven other apartments in the same building, four on each floor, two on each corner..

While Sara is working on a spreadsheet, Bruce put together a document with all the apartments in our zone, together with the names of the missionaries occupying them, the wards in their area, and address and phone numbers. We will be inspecting the two furthest north, with Petersons and Martins inspecting two each as well. He shared the document with the other couples, hoping they will comment on any errors or omissions. Sara got 100% on a case study.

Sister Chun, Sara and Bruce, Elder Garner, and Elder Gilmore (photographer) chez Destinn

Elder Gilmore will be flying home in just under a week, so Destinn threw a party for him. Sara put together a great salad and a fruit plate (which we forgot to photograph), and there was steak and baked potatoes and other food. It was a feast. We arrived in time to help set up, but left earlier than others. On the way home, we stopped at Safeway to buy seven cases of hint water that were on sale and on top of that 50% off. Home and in bed after nine. A long day.

Saturday morning, Sara was the first one up, while Bruce slept in until just after seven. After the bank opened, he drove there to withdraw some cash, in case Jasmin called to replace our rear brakes (he didn't) and drove to Destinn's to see if he needed help cleaning up (he didn't).

Sara got 100% on another quiz. We drove to Popeye's to join the district for lunch at 2pm. But, they had just finished eating. Apparently they changed the time to 1pm and no one had told us! Our feelings are hurt—we thought they liked us.

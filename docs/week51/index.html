Hawaii Missions — Week 51

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (June 7, 2026) morning, awake early, realizing: this is our last Sunday in the Hawaii Honolulu Mission (HHM). This is reflected in the generalized title of this week's post, acknowledging that this week we will be serving in two missions, with the changeover happening sometime on Thursday. Bruce also learned that our former ward in Salt Lake City has changed names — and stakes — because the stake we served in was discontinued last November, being absorbed into two neighboring stakes. This kind of change he finds unsettling, but expects he will just have to learn to adapt; it is certainly humbling. Meanwhile, Sara went on a drive with her children around sunrise. Jessica is planning to attend fast and testimony meeting with us today. While waiting for the time to roll around (meeting starts at 11:30am), Bruce noticed our one new picture on the wall, and chose this photo to document the changes.

This gives the room a different vibe; we still need to decide what to do about the cutout words on the shelf: Seal, Beach, Ocean. At 4pm, Bruce went to a baptism; Harley at the stake center.

Elder Walker, Harley Godwin, Elder Garner

Where's Harley? (covered in leis and surrounded by young friends); Bruce sixth from right

Sara needed to sleep. When she awoke, well after Bruce returned, she was at first confused, and thought that he hadn't left for the baptism yet. Jessica had walked to Haleiwa Joe's and called for her to drive over there to meet her, and she did so while Bruce took a turn napping.

They sent him a picture showing how happy they were, accompanied by the message, "It's not too late to join the party!!!!" And while Bruce certainly could have walked there to join in the festivities, he elected to start sleeping early, for tomorrow will be a tremendously long and hard day of moving furniture. The furniture is primarily a new king-sized bed for us, so there is that.

Bruce wasted some time getting worked up with worry about how we're going to get everything done tomorrow, and Sara calmed him down. We'll likely be asleep shortly after ten. Long day.

Monday morning, we woke up early to get ready for the moving day. It started by Bruce going out to the pickup truck and carrying in three king-sized pillows and the rest of the bedding that we had brought a few days ago when we staged for this move. Then he carefully removed the tarp that had been protecting the four bicycles from the rain. After breakfast and showers, we all squeezed into the pickup cab and drove to the nearby UHaul franchisee. Sara then drove the box truck with Jessica, and they followed Bruce in the pickup to the Banyan Tree Plaza. Bruce kept going, around the corner to the mission office, where he had help unloading the bicycles (the assistants to the president happened to be arriving and he thought they were coming to help him, and they were kind enough not to correct him, and just help). About that time, the Mangums also came down to help. With that done, Bruce drove the empty pickup around the block to the Banyan Tree Plaza and helped/bossed the moving of all kinds of furniture.

There was a bit of a mixup about where the wooden desk needed to be loaded, and the elders already had it in the box truck when Bruce realized that. He ordered Jessica and Sara to come down and deliver the (sold) blue sofa (which had also been loaded into the box truck and so had to be taken out and put in the pickup first). When they returned with the (once again) empty pickup, Bruce backed it up to the box truck and the elders slid the big desk into the bed of the pickup and he tied it down for the trip to Kaneohe. Sister Vogelsberg had baked some muffins and gave them to the elders, who were quite pleased and posed for a couple of pictures before we left, again with Bruce leading the way in the pickup and the ladies following in the box truck.

Vogelsbergs and then us; front: Elders Nathan, Niemann, Cannon; back: McIff, Bean, Wayment

When we rolled back into Haiku Point, Bruce phoned Elders Gilmore and Burton who were just finishing their lunch and came over in five minutes. They had been eating $5 McDonalds breakfasts at Golden Pho because they didn't want to spend the money on pho like the other elders had, so they were done already. They didn't have a lot of time, but they carried in the pieces of the king-sized bed — and helped assemble it — before they had to drive to exchange cars at the Honolulu West stake center (the mission has recently received several new cars).

Next came the part Bruce had dreaded: taking the extra furniture to the dump. Well, it turns out that Haiku Point has a bulky items policy for garbage, and they will store it away for us until the first of the month when the county sends garbage trucks around to pick up bulky items. We had just missed the June pickup by hours, so Aaron, Jasmin, and Supila coaxed the wooden office desk out of the pickup bed. "The thing weighs a ton," Jasmin exaggerated. So, since they will have to carry everything to the curb next month, Aaron decided to break it down into pieces and went for a sledge hammer! Which they used along with electric screwdrivers to take it apart.

First with just pieces of the desk, and then with the dining set thrown in; sad no one wanted it

We did manage to find homes for some of the smaller items, and Supila wanted a small table.

Namele came by to pick up the twin bed frame we've been keeping for her for a couple of weeks (from Danny's bed). And she wanted one of the small tables, too. And we are holding a small chest of drawers for the elders who helped us unload, and they will come pick it up when they have their new car.

We drove the box truck to get gas and then returned it to the UHaul franchisee, leaving inside the two brown love seats, because one of their employees wants them for her mother. The whole trip cost $87 for the rental, plus $23 for gas, both of which the mission will reimburse. Bruce carried in a few things we had put in the back of the cab of the pickup truck, and then drove it to fill it with gas ($45) and park it on the street, none the worse for wear, he hopes. While doing this, he took a phone call from his good friend, Jacob Siebach, who has arrived in Hawaii for a few days of work, but can't see his daughter. So hard. We will get together for sure.

As Bruce is writing this paragraph, lying on the king-sized bed, his head resting against the padded headboard, and with his laptop on his chest, his heart swells with gratitude that that part of the day is now safely in the past. Thank you, Lord. And we are so grateful to the many elders who gave up part of their precious preparation day to help us.

Tuesday morning, Bruce sent Melinda a text message wishing her a happy birthday, after failing to reach her by phone. We fixed breakfast, showered, and are ready to attend the weekly district council meeting. They decided to start with a breakfast and so we purchased some fruit trays at Safeway on our way to the meeting. The meeting was good, and ended with an inspiring testimony meeting. They took a picture afterwards and we're going to miss most of them.

Sisters Naophiankham, Arancon; Elders Maguire, Garner, Gilmore, Burton, Walker, Frogley; us

Only Elders Garner, Gilmore, and Walker will remain, and we will likely be in a district with only one of them, so there'll be 7 new missionaries to meet, come next district council meeting.

After the meeting and Sara having finished washing and putting away the dishes, we drove home to pick up Jessica and then up to the Polynesian Cultural Center where she bought us all (discounted) passes to The Islands. Before going in, we purchased lunch at Tita's Grill.

Jessica, us, Helaman (May 27th at Laenani Beach); us at Tita's; Jessica and us in The Islands

We started by taking a one way canoe ride from the near end to the far end of the area, and then walked back to the entrance. We met up with Jacob, who popped out of an exhibit as we passed by. We attended a presentation by Samoans. On the way to our car we ran into Sister Lowry of the other (soon to be our) mission who promised to forward the mission newsletter to us (which she did). Puzzle day, with Sara finishing sudoku long before Bruce did.

Wednesday, the day ended with us sneaking out of the house to go eat at the other pho place.

Sara with a bowl of shrimp pho and Bruce with calamari and an avocado shake

The picture is an awkward collage* of two not-so-well-taken selfies combining the best view of the photographer with the best view of the food. [An avocado dessert —  inspired by Rachel, our niece's oldest child, who tried one recently while getting ready for her mission to Vietnam.]

Wednesday began in the morning, as Sara and Jessica left at 5am to go to Lanikai Beach, where they took in the water and then got out because of jellyfish therein. We ate breakfast and are getting ready to take Sara to Doctor Lim, about her knee, at 10:30. When we arrived they wanted to repeat the x-rays, but we insisted that they get the images from the hospital (where they had been taken earlier) and they were able to get the images, but that delayed our being seen until nearly noon. Meanwhile, Helaman was picking up Alyssa at the airport. Bruce and Sara got lunch at Costco and tried to get new tires, but they don't carry the size we need. We ran into Sister Cann from our soon-to-be mission (and her nephew and his wife) and had a nice long talk with them. When we got home, Bruce made an appointment at Lex Brodies to get new tires the day after tomorrow. Bruce finished the crossword puzzle first. The two of us went out just before sunset and ate at the Pho Station, where the food was good (as shown earlier ("begin with the end in mind" wasn't what Covey meant) to fit the picture better).

Thursday we got up at seven and hit the road to the airport by 7:20am. Our mission: to fetch two elders from the airport to the tabernacle. We got them there just after nine (the goal was before ten, so we achieved that). The two we picked up are going to serve in the Mililani zone. We are now officially serving in the Hawaii Laie Mission. Our marching orders were issued by the HHM but were to facilitate the transferring in of elders who will be serving with us in the HLM.

The Hawaii Laie Mission (HLM) is now the northern ½  (a bit more really) of the island of Oahu

The decision to realign the mission boundaries was finalized in May, but didn't take effect for us until today, June 11, 2026 (which also happens to be a state holiday, King Kamehameha Day).

We all ate at Haleiwa Joes, after three of us got there at 4pm to stand in line for a reservation.

Selfies by Jessica, then by Bruce (having taken lessons from her); Keahi a Kahoe from our table

At our table: Alyssa, Jessica, Sara, Bruce, and Helaman (who took the selfie)

At 7pm, Bruce went to the family search session we host Thursdays, a little bit late. Long day.

Friday morning we got up early (before 5am). Sara had wanted to go to the ocean but decided she was too tired and went back to bed. Bruce stayed up to work on this blog post. We drove to Lex Brodies for the 8:30 appointment; they checked the tread and advised us to wait six months to a year. Came home and Helaman and Jessica gave Bruce his birthday presents: a beautiful Aloha shirt and a lovely basket of plums. So happy. The three younger people went on an expedition to a beach and their elders napped. At 2pm, Bruce and Sara jumped in the Scion and drove out to the street; in a few minutes, the pickup parked behind them, the young people piled into the back seat, and Bruce got us all to the Pearl Harbor National Memorial in time for our 2:30 appointment to ride the boat out to the USS Arizona Memorial. A very moving place.

Back on shore we watched the movie. Then we drove to Da Seafood Cartel for dinner.

We got home, leaving Jessica just enough time to change and pack for her flight home. Bruce and Sara drove her to the airport, leaving at 7:15pm and dropping her off at departures at 7:42pm. After leaving the airport, we took one exit too soon, and were about to enter a military base, but found a detour around it and got back to Kaneohe, ordered a couple of tacos at Jack in the Box, then finally got home to sleep. Another long day.

Saturday morning, we woke up early. Bruce went back to sleep, while Sara texted with Jessica, who had had some problems boarding her flight to SLC (they didn't want her to take food onto the flight (how strange!)). Now that Jessica is gone — and she is thoroughly missed — we are rearranging the furniture, a natural succession of events given the new king-sized bed that came into our life this week. Plus, we are doing a lot of laundry. Bruce phoned Melinda and learned about their 25th anniversary cruise later this month, to Spain, Italy, Greece, etc. ending with four days at Lago di Como in Italy where they will renew their vows. So romantic. Also, her son, Jacob, will be baptized and we'll be able to attend remotely over zoom tomorrow afternoon. That was a bit of a surprise; Bruce needs to keep in better — more frequent at least — touch with his family. Alyssa and Helaman are helping Sara prepare lunch, starring leftovers of prime rib from eating out earlier this same week. It has been a full and busy week.

Bruce is attempting to produce a clearer map of the new Hawaii Laie Mission, by combining maps of the Mililani Stake and the Kaneohe Stake into a single map of the island of Oahu. The new HLM is the old (inverted L-shaped) mission along the north shore, plus the two stakes.

Another awkward collage: old Laie mission at top; Mililani stake below left; Kaneohe below right

Helaman made sandwiches from leftover prime rib and ciabatti rolls, purchased yesterday at Safeway, and Alyssa made a fine shrimp-laden hand-made ceviche. After this marvelous lunch, naps ensued. Helaman and Alyssa took our pickle ball paddles and went out, presumably in search of a court to play on. Sara and Bruce spent a lot more time moving around smaller pieces of furniture and dealing with tons of paperwork, but finally ready for bed.

We are ending our weekly report with this card that Jessica presented to Sara the last day.

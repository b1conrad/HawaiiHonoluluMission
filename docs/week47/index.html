Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 47

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (May 10, 2026) and it is Mother's Day. Bruce had been asked to pray in sacrament meeting. There were four youth talks, a primary song, and a talk from Elder Burton. In the priesthood meeting we all shared how we got our names, something about our mother, and our favorite calling. Eli called disappointed about a bad thing that happened to him. Ellen texted the photograph of the returned missionary we met on Friday night.

Bruce, Sara, the returned missionary who served in Taber, Sister Otukolo

In the afternoon of Sunday, we left at 3:30pm to go and pick up Denise and Danny and then drove them to Aiea, to the apartment of the Andersens who are leaving for home in just over a week, and have to get rid of all the furnishings in their apartment (because they are not being replaced). They liked a number of things which we piled into our car. But then Bruce took a wrong turn, onto H3, and we ended up in Kaneohe. So, we had something to eat in the food court of the Windward Mall, taking a couple of pictures there at the provided backdrop.

Then we drove them home, arriving just before sunset. We helped them up the stairs with their things and left just after sunset, arriving home at 7:20pm. So, another long day.

Monday we got up at 6am and got dressed. We have an important meeting in Honolulu at 8:30am and are deciding how much time to allow to get there. Helaman agrees to allow an hour, so getting breakfast and then hitting the road. We arrived in plenty of time, and the outcome was okay. The next hearing will be on July 13. We shopped at Costco, including two pizzas for dinner with the elders tonight. We drove home and unloaded everything, and then Bruce drove to sell back some empty bottles and cans and buy some pantothenic acid to treat what we think he is suffering from, shingles. He and Sara drove the pizzas to the fridge in the stake center because they didn't fit in ours.

Big news. That will affect the title of this blog! Our zone in Kaneohe, along with the zone of Mililani, will become part of the Hawaii Laie Mission, effective June 11. So we were called to one mission, but will return home from a different mission. Not terribly uncommon, as the church will be opening 55 new missions effective July 1, anyway. Big news announcement concludes.

Sara took a nap, and Bruce drove to Kailua to pick up a book requested at the library, and drop off some empty canning jars with Kevin. Upon his return, we both drove to Safeway to purchase some desert and garlic bread to add to the pizzas we got from Costco. The six elders were at the Stake Center when we went to the kitchen to doctor up the pizzas. All six came to our house a half hour later for an energetic dinner. They left us a message and returned to work. We'll be asleep shortly after nine. Another long and eventful day.

Tuesday morning, Bruce had had a difficult night because he was trying to avoid sleeping on his left shoulder, which we discovered last night had a horrible rash, explaining the pain he had felt.

We (meaning Sara) put antibiotic ointment on it. Even though he didn't feel very well, he went to the district council meeting. Sara didn't go because without hot water she was unable to wash her hair, plus a plumber was scheduled to come during the meeting. The meeting was the usual inspiration. It's a good thing he went because Namele came at 10:30am — as arranged — to rehearse the musical number with him. However, he forgot to bring the paperwork we had promised the missionaries (it was in Sara's purse, so would have automatically been at the meeting (had she attended)), so the missionaries stopped by later in the day to pick it up.

Wednesday, we received another picture from Ellen:

Sisters Roapamoa and Otukolo, taken January 31, 2025 at John and Ellen's home

It was fun to see their things in the background and realize that this actually happened even before we knew we would need to live in Hawaii, and before we drove up to Canada to visit with them (all as reported in our week zero post) before our mission began. End of flashback.

We left for Doctor Sharon Lawler's office about 8:30am and got Bruce checked in as a new patient, and patiently waited for her to arrive. She immediately identified the problem as shingles and prescribed an anti-viral and a nerve-specific pain killer. We then drove to the Andersen's house to pick up a desk and chair and a few other things and took them to Denise (with Helaman providing the transportation with his pickup truck). After that, we caravanned to Betty's Burgers for lunch, and then came home to rest, stopping just at the pharmacy. We got home about 2:30pm, so it felt like a long day again.

Thursday morning, Bruce was happy to have slept well, but was a little light headed when he woke up a few times during the night, a known side effect of the drug he is taking against the pain, Gabapentin (300mg). The other drug, Acyclovir (400mg), is an anti-viral. Things seem to be improving slowly. Bruce delivered a package to Elder Garner and got back in time for our weekly zoom call called SYS.

Sister and President Andersen, with him speaking with a great message as per usual

Later in the meeting, we took a picture of Sister Siebach saying the closing prayer and sent it to her father, our friend Jacob (who you, dear reader, will hear more about, when he visits Hawaii (and us, briefly) early next month). Diedra (who represents us so well with our health plans (and we highly recommend her)) phoned so that the person (or was it a robot?) could work through the process of getting new ID cards mailed to us, which took well over 15 minutes. She (it?) needed to hear our voices repeat what Diedra had just told her (name, birthday, address, phone number). Interestingly, we heard Diedra say them, so anyone could have pretended to be us. Strange sort of security. The plumber is here, replacing the water heater it looks like. We had expected him to come inside to replace the faucets in our bathtub. And, he did that later, replacing just the hot water faucet. Later, we napped and Sara bathed (finally with enough hot water). At 6pm we arrived first to the stake center and unlocked the gates and the doors. No one came, but at 6:30pm, Bruce joined the ward gospel study group for a stimulating discussion. At home, Bruce fixed his pico engine which had run out of disk space this afternoon and he just noticed it, while watching the movie Darkest Hour, ending before 10:30pm.

Friday came. Phone call with Sue Green, a friend from our distant past. We are mostly lounging around, Bruce with his shirt off to give his skin some air; he also sat out in the sun for a few minutes to harvest some vitamin D. About 2:45pm there was a bolt of lightning immediately followed by thunder and the power went out. The electric storm was right over us for about half an hour but has moved on now. We were supposed to help Destinn prepare for the party tomorrow, but he wasn't comfortable with Bruce having shingles around his granddaughter. We are using Helaman's Jackery box to provide USB power for Bruce's iPhone which is providing Internet access for us, while we wait for power to return. Not expecting it very soon because there are multiple outages on the island. The power came back on a couple of hours later.

Saturday we didn't have much to do. In the afternoon we went to Destinn's place to help him set up chairs and the food serving area and so forth. There was live entertainment.

There were lots more people there. Bruce couldn't stand the pain any longer, so we left right after filling a plate; we would have left earlier, but he insisted we take a plate of food. Fun fact, the bishopric councilor texted and asked Bruce to speak in church tomorrow... Never rains.

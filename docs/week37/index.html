Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 37

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (March 1, 2026) morning, Sara put together a lunch for our mission leaders, with help from Bruce. Then, while she took a bath, he drove it over to the Waikalua chapel, arriving at 10:30am, which turned out to be just right. Because — apparently — they keep the kitchen locked during services on Sundays. So, he got the door unlocked, put the package in the fridge and pulled closed the locked door behind him. Then he messaged Sister Andersen and the entire zone, alerting them to the lockdown situation. He returned to pick up Sara for our fast and testimony meeting, which was great. Just before it started, Bruce delivered a letter to Sister Krupp, and asked her to make sure they got the food. After the meeting, we met the Stokers visiting from Waikiki who had a raft of questions about serving a mission and we had a nice talk with them. We ate and napped, and at 4:15 we drove over to the other chapel to make sure the kitchen door was unlocked, and it was, and the package of food was no longer in the fridge; Bruce then stopped worrying about it. He signed up for seminary security, not this Monday but the next one, on Colleen's birthday.

Picture taken by Helaman the previous week (because the blog needs a picture above the fold).

Monday morning, Sara was awake at 4am, but Bruce slept until 6:25 and then joined the meeting with Phil's students. Low-key day, eating, reading, napping.

Tuesday morning, we were up around 1am, hoping to see the total lunar eclipse, but the sky remained overcast the few times Bruce went outside to check. So we missed that one. We ate breakfast and got dressed and got to district council at 10am. It was great as usual. We brought along a pull-up bar for one of the elders, which had arrived on Monday, and had them come out to our car to get it as we left. We then drove straight to Costco in Waipio, where we filled the gas tank, ate a hot dog and slice of pizza, and bought a lot of groceries. At home we brought everything in and put it away, then drove to Safeway to buy some donuts for Jasmin, and some acidophilus for us. As we delivered the former, some people who were downsizing brought out a couple of things (mirror and side table) that we accepted and put in our car. Then home to work the weekly puzzles (Sara did better than Bruce) and nap. Long talk with Helaman.

Wednesday came as days will do. After a leisurely breakfast, we drove to the beach.

180° pseudo panorama from North to South via East. Sara is the small figure near the right.

The rest of the day was pretty laid back, talking, eating, napping, reading, etc.

Thursday we got up (as we do); Bruce read for awhile on the porch until driven inside by a bit of rain, while Sara talked with Eli. After breakfast, we attended the SYS zoom call. In the afternoon, Bruce drove to Kailua, selling cans and bottles for $5.15, picking up up some library books, and returning some things to Amazon at Whole Foods. In the evening, we drove to the stake center, where Sara staffed the genealogy library while Bruce guided a discussion on the prophet Moroni. The full moon was visible as we were preparing for bed.

This was a day and a half too late for the total eclipse, but nice to see the moon!

Friday we had a bucket of water overflow beside the air conditioner in our bedroom. Sometimes — exactly how or why we don't know — it becomes (also) a dehumidifier and issues water out of a hose into a bucket. When we realize it, we (of course) empty the bucket. We hope we caught it before the apartment downstairs was impacted. A lot of towels ended up being used, then washed and dried. In the afternoon, we shopped the deals at Safeway, and put a lot of pork into the freezer. Sara also cut up seven pounds or so of beef tri-tip, most of which ended up in the freezer. From a pound of fat, we rendered some beef tallow overnight in the slow cooker. Bruce read the last book of a sci-fi trilogy by Cixin Liu (translated from Chinese by Ken Liu).

Saturday morning, Bruce got up just before sunrise and finished the book, while marveling at the beautiful colors in the fluffy clouds. Sara had some phone conversations with children, friends, and medical people (scheduling an MRI). Bruce just checked a bank account and saw with relief that annual insurance premiums had been paid; he then sat contemplating things that happen once a year, and things that happen once a month, and things that happen once a week, and things that happen once a day, and the rhythm of life in general. The new book of checks (a new one must be ordered every two years) arrived a couple of days ago, and he will be making out the one that goes to the church to pay tithing and our mission monthly rent soon.

Sara suffered with vertigo most of the day, although we did manage to have a couple of good meals. She made an appointment with Kevin for tomorrow morning at 9:30am in Kailua.

Monday morning, we awoke early, as Bruce has a meeting with Phil's students over zoom at 7am. Taking Sara to her appointment in Kailua. While she was there, Bruce went to the library (for one thing, for a parking space) to get some books, and work on a little thing for the demo that Phil's students are preparing. Afterwards, we ate lunch at Whole Foods and picked up some prescription medication on the way home. In a few minutes, Bruce will be going to the stake center to open the family search room for the young missionaries on their P-day. Bruce finished a gadget for Phil's students to use in their demo (see the read-me file). Phil gave him a thank you emoji, so that is rewarding (he will work for praise).

Tuesday we prepared a salad and took it and a bunch of things to the chapel so that we could serve lunch to our mission leaders during the break in their interviews. Laundry emergency: ink spill — there was a bic pen in a shirt pocket! In the evening, Bruce liked the sunlight on Keahi a Kahoe (usually cloudy) and took a picture of it (which wasn't as colorful as being there).

Wednesday we attended district council, which ended with a lesson from Elder Gilmore.

We then delivered a card to the Chuns, and dropped off empty cans and bottles. Home for a game of Skip Bo, and naps. And the day unwound to a close.

Thursday our screen door was flung wide open by the strong winds and broke and we're contacting the property management people to have it replaced; lots of wind here, banging the door open and closed, since it no longer latches, because it binds at the bottom on the hinge side and is swelling from humidity and becoming too wide at the bottom.

Inset showing wear at hinge side; door thrown wide open; inset showing breaks at latch side

Late breakfast, then at 10am, the SYS meeting was uplifting. Lunch and naps ensued. Helaman helped Bruce remove the door from its hinges and we laid it down sideways to prevent further damage. In the evening we both went to host in the FamilySearch center, but had no patrons; we struggled with the concept and consequences of private persons, and Sara got her Bruce connected to his (public) ancestors, which was fun. She also added her persons for his previous spouses and biological children, which was interesting. Sara wrote to Ellen asking about this.

Friday morning, Bruce woke up to find Sara had gone out to the couch, presumably to avoid waking him up with the light, and then fallen asleep there. Ellen, meanwhile, had answered our question from yesterday. We went out to shop at Safeway and decided that we like not having a screen door. Home with groceries for tonight's meal with four elders and doing a lot of preparation for that. A plumber came to look at our tub faucets which leak; he will make an estimate and plan a water shutoff and repair sometime in the future. He spoke with Aaron, the manager here, about it and then drove off. The faucets still work but we have to tighten them superhumanly hard to prevent a dripping leak. We napped and then prepared the meal. It was so great having the four elders; they gave an inspiring message about the mission of Amulek. Just think, a couple of years ago they were priests in their wards, and now they are stalwart elders. So great to see that, and to have another opportunity to tell our stories about Mauritius and earlier times.

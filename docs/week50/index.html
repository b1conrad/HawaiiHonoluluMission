Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 50

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (May 31, 2026) marks the end of another calendar month, leaving us 7 months of mission. The last post ended on a cliffhanger, with Sara suffering. During the night she took another dose of Azo and a couple more drinks of bwa de ron, and says she feels fine this morning. That was about 5:30 am, and now, at 7am she is still sleeping having gone back to bed. Bruce is hoping for the best and preparing for the morning session of stake conference. He sang with the choir, which was white shirt and tie, so he folded up his gray suit jacket. The talks were all very good. At the end, we all stood to sing aloha oe to the departing authority.

Cropped still from video (at 2:51:34); stake presidency and visitor and wife in leis on left

Bruce is second from left in back row of the men's section, and had put on his suit jacket by then. The visiting authority is Elder James R. Rasband (of the seventy (first cousin once removed of the apostle of the same family name)). The Longs gave Bruce a ride home, and gifted us an aloe vera plant (from which to extract gel to soothe Sara's condition), along with the most beautiful pop-up card and a couple of gifts.

Monday morning we both feel a little bit better. For Bruce the cold is progressing as it usually does, but he feels pretty listless. For Sara, the pain of her shingles is mostly gone. We are now trying to figure out how to pay the tuition for Sara's next class, which will be through BYU Pathway Worldwide. Sara is working on organizing a truck rental from U-Haul and a schedule to pick up the king size bed from Honolulu tomorrow. Looks like that's not going to work out. Naps. Helaman and Jessica went on an expedition (to Kapolei (and a beach somewhere)) and Bruce and Sara watched some shows. Jessica thinks she has shingles on her leg. Bruce drove to the stake center to open up the family search room for one set of elders.

Tuesday morning, we are both feeling better. Bruce got up, showered, and dressed, and we fixed breakfast. Our next event is the weekly district council meeting at 10am, and then we'll drive into Honolulu to help clear out the Lelegren's apartment as today is their last day, and it needs to be empty. We bought lunch at Safeway then got to Honolulu twenty minutes early, so we parked and ate, and then drove to the apartment building. View of tabernacle from 12th floor.

Daniel was there too, along with the Vogelsbergs and the Lelegrens. Daniel and Bruce took apart the bed and staged it for pickup another day. Daniel was able to relieve them of all the leftover food, etc. We drove home a load of stuff, mostly for donation, and rested for a bit. Then Sara and her kids went out to eat. Rounding out a busy day, the weekly puzzles: Sara finished sudoku first, while Bruce was first to be done with crossword.

Wednesday morning and after breakfast just resting. Skip Bo. At 11:30am, we met Daniel and his daughter, Karah, at Pan's Kitchen where we shared lunch. Bruce got a call from the sisters, one of whom had just sprained her ankle, so we split up: the ladies went back to our house to go through the things we had brought from Honolulu yesterday, and Daniel drove Bruce to Longs to buy an Ace Bandage. We took it to their house and Daniel wrapped the ankle. Then we met up at our house and continued going through things. Danel and Bruce had a good discussion about Daniel's questions, including infant baptism (Bruce later sent him the reference of Moroni chapter 8 to read, which he said he would). Bruce finished cleaning out the pickup truck and took two boxes of things to the donation center, then parked the truck on the street. Another call from the sisters, and Bruce and Sara drove back to Longs to buy a set of crutches, which we delivered and helped the sisters assemble. On the way home, we learned that Jessica had walked to the mall, where Sara went to join her (and the farmers' market) while Bruce went down for a nap. Later they returned with some fruit and bread. Sister Hovey phoned and asked for help in the Family Search center on Saturday, for a Relief Society activity. Another long day, ending with a bath for Sara at 9pm.

Thursday, we are up at the crack of dawn to get ready for a big day. We have a new stove and oven coming this morning, and before that a handyman to move our fridge out of the way (then after the installation, the handyman will return to put the fridge back into place). He just moved it, and it was a big deal, including having to replace the shutoff valve. As we prepare to change to a bigger bed, Bruce took (before) pictures of our ocean-themed bedroom walls.

Watch for pictures to be taken after everything is rearranged. We are now waiting for the SYS zoom call, to be followed by delivery of the new stove/oven. After the stove delivery, they called to find out that it had been delivered and were going to have Larry (the handyman) come back, but his schedule won't allow that until after 3:30pm All four of us drove out to get some food. We had planned to eat at the botanical garden, but the rain was too heavy, so we just brought the food home to eat. Larry came just before 4pm and had the fridge back in place and in operation in just a few minutes. Bruce gave him two boxes of prepared food (MRE and emergency both) and he left happy that we are happy with his work. Text from Daniel that his car was repoed. Sara is still sleeping. We played (part of) a game of Skip Bo, and then left for the stake center, running into Jessica and Helaman who were just returning from the eye doctor; her vision is okay. Bruce took along his computer because we have a zoom meeting with our next mission president midway through our Family Search session. Waiting for the zoom meeting to start. Before it we met Ryan Wright from our ward who came in and logged into FS. We got to know our next mission leaders who seem very nice, and began to learn their mission culture, including that we are still invited to the weekly district council meetings. Sara suggested Bruce write to the mission leaders to ask if he could teach a class at BYU Hawaii. Home via supper at Panda Express, and to finish the Skip Bo game which Sara won handily.

Friday morning Sara asked Bruce to set an alarm so she could take Jessica to a beach to watch the sun rise. Meanwhile, Bruce is enjoying the view from the porch, in a limpid breeze as the temperature very gradually goes up in the sunlight that makes it through a fluffy cloud layer.

Keahi a Kahoe without the usual cloud layer obscuring the summit

They came back with breakfast, having had a great experience on an uncrowded Kailua beach where the water was bathtub warmth. Sara loved it. This fruit is called mountain apple.

On the outside it looks a lot like an apple, although it is pear-shaped. It tastes a lot like an asian pear but the texture is more like a ripe bartlett pear. Instead of a core of seeds, though, it has a single large pit (shown in cross section).

In the afternoon, Sara and Jessica are driving to the Dole pineapple factory, and got gas along the way. Jessica prepared a supper of salmon, asparagus, and rice.

Saturday morning Bruce awoke just before sunrise and went to sit on the porch. Jessica was awake and so was first to wish him a happy birthday. Messages had come in overnight from his high school friend, John, and from a cousin, and from his sister, Myrna.

Keahi a Kahoe this time with the usual cloud layer obscuring the summit

Bruce had a piece of birthday pie for breakfast. Very good. We are now preparing for the Relief Society activity in which we play a small part in the Family Search room. It was super chaotic, yet rewarding. Elder Frogley called needing crutches, so we went and bought him a pair. Helaman went with Bruce to pick up two bicycles in Waimanalo and two in Kaneohe, as requested by the Mangums, so that we can bring them to Honolulu Monday morning in the empty truck. Jessica prepared a birthday supper for Bruce which he much appreciated. The day ended with a sweet text from Bridget.

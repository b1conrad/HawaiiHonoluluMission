Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

We had a nice talk with Nadine Frost in the parking lot before leaving for home, rest, lunch, naps. This afternoon, we’ll be returning to the church for Donna’s baptism at 5pm, where Bruce snapped this candid.

Monday morning Bruce got up to go sit in the chair on the porch in the early morning sunshine. He talked with Elizabeth on the phone and also briefly with Bridget who was accepted into dance and theater camps this summer. Elizabeth made a transcript of Bruce’s talk “so it wouldn’t be lost.” He learned that Ashlyn is in Calgary and will get an apartment near downtown on the 1st with a great view from the balcony. Bruce looked up Jessica's flight and noted that she is now over halfway to San Diego on her way here, to arrive at 9pm today. SkipBo and breakfast. Lunch, laundry, naps. We drove to pick her up at the airport just before 9pm and are safely home with her and her luggage.

Tuesday came. Bruce spent a few minutes on the deck; Helaman drove to a doctor appointment; Jessica started an on-line work meeting; we retired to our bedroom. Phone calls with Janis and then with Eli. After district council, we bought lunch at Safeway. Jessica was in another meeting so we ate in our bedroom. Now, the ladies are going shopping at Costco in Waipahu, including gas. In the evening, we drove some watermelon and cheesecake to the Chuns and to Destinn. The latter wasn't home and we gave his to his neighbor, Karl. Home and in bed.

Wednesday morning, Bruce awoke first and went to sit outside but Jessica beat him to it, but soon came in and he took in the outside air and finished a book and ignored a little bit of rian. When he came back in, Sara and Jessica were talking on the couch. We met the sisters for lunch at Golden Pho, because they won a competition in the district; the losers will wash our car someday.

Jessica, Sara, Bruce, Sister Arancon, Sister Naophiankham sharing lunch on the patio

Funny story: when the appetizers came (before the picture), the three of us began to eat, but the sisters didn't; Bruce finally figured it out and asked Sister Naophiankham to say a blessing on the food. That got things going. After lunch, they gave us a message, based on Mosiah 4:15, and asked us each to share what we learned from our parents. We were pleased to hear from Jessica.

On the way home we drove north around the coast, then picked up Helaman and kept going, all the way to Keneke's, stopping at a couple of beaches along the way. Bruce took off his shoes and socks, rolled up his pant legs and waded a bit. We stopped at a couple of fruit stands and bought a lot of fruits. Bruce finished both of the weekly puzzles first, a first.

Thursday, we enjoyed an early morning, followed by sleeping in a bit but then rushing to get everything ready for a handyman to come and move the fridge before the delivery men come to install the new oven. After all that, it turns out that it is not this Thursday, but next Thursday. Oh well, we've had a dress rehearsal. Just after two we took Jessica and dropped her off near the beach in Waikiki, then drove to Safeway near Denise's place for a lesson with Elder Nathan and Elder Cannon, which went well. Then we drove her and her son home and met Jessica and Helaman at Savers where we did a little shopping. Home just in time for Helaman and Jessica to drop Bruce off at the stake center to man the family search session. Sara picked him up at 8pm. A long day, and tomorrow promises to start early, as Sara has a doctor's appointment at 8am.

Friday we woke up about 5:30 spontaneously although Bruce had set an alarm for 6:30. We left earlier than planned, arriving at the doctors 7:30. The doctor says it is shingles and prescribed Acyclovir and sent her to an eye doctor upstairs. From whence, this view of Diamond Head.

Her eyesight has not been affected. We drove back to Kaneohe and picked up Helaman and Jessica and drove to Safeway, where they shopped while Bruce tried to get prescriptions at Target and then went to pick up books held for him in Kaneohe library. He failed at the former, so after picking them up, Sara obtained the prescriptions at Longs. Home for naps and reading and laundry. Three of us are planning to watch a movie at the mall at 7:30pm. We watched The Devil Wears Prada 2 in theater; we being Sara, Jessica, and Bruce. Got home and will be sleeping soon, let's say at 10:30pm. Sara is trying very hard to pay tuition for her BYU Pathways class, but … website woes.

Saturday morning came (Bruce was going to say early (but that can't happen)) and we are thinking of going to a beach to snort some salt water. Bruce received a phone call from Elizabeth, who is in Lehi preparing for her nephew Trevin's wedding next weekend. Discussion ensued about which beach to go to; it being Saturday the popular ones will be crowded. We drove to Bellows beach and stayed there for about three hours; the surf was quite energetic. Sadly, it pulled off Sara's wedding band, the one that Ammon made — matching ones of for us both over twenty years ago. This was sad. Jessica felt a bit responsible and bought her a replacement band near the food trucks. We stopped at the food trucks on the way out and had some good and relatively inexpensive food. Then home to bathe (or at least rinse off the salt) and nap.

When Bruce woke up, there was just a few minutes before we needed to leave for the Saturday evening session of stake conference. But Sara was severely suffering from what looks like a UTI. He reminded her that we have some bwa de ron here and she prepared some of that and he ran off to attend the conference. When he walked in, the stake president asked him to take a sandwich (of which there were many remaining from food brought in for after the leadership session), so he wolfed one down. He met the new senior couple, the Martins, who were arriving and introduced them to the president, and to Elder Kaonohi (service mission couple over family history and employment, who just have one month left on their term). He then sat next to the Longs, who provided himi with information about how to get a prescription for antibiotics (which urgent care center, unfortunately won't be open until 8am tomorrow morning).

We had a nice talk with the Longs, who gifted us another aloe vera plant, and arrived home in time to sign in to Zoom for Jacob's baptism! At 2:30pm our time.

Monday morning we enjoyed sleeping in for a little bit. Bruce wrote to our new mission secretary asking some questions. One of the answers is that our former mission president needs to have someone in the missionary department make the change so that our missionary portal shows the correct mission for us. Bruce made an appointment with Jung Oh, to help tomorrow with an issue in the BYU Hawaii IT department, before Jung leaves on a trip to California.

We did some shopping in the afternoon at Longs and at Safeway. Watching a movie, One Man Against the Coup. And then, a video presentation on the Universal Model, during which Sara fell asleep. She woke up to a call from Eli.

Tuesday morning, we both awoke spontaneously at 6:30am. Sara took a call with Jessica while Bruce filled out his ballot for the election. We need to mail them today, which Bruce did as Sara  began fixing breakfast. Bruce built a transfer calendar tab for the next six weeks, copying from the PDF calendar that we had received. Preparing to attend our first "outbound" district council meeting in our new mission. The Laie Mission has mostly sisters, who mostly work in the temple visitor center, but have an "outbound" experience in proselyting for four to six months; that will happen in either the Kaneohe or Mililani zone, which is why our district council is characterized as outbound. We're assuming it is going to be in the stake center, but will remain flexible. Both districts were there, and we joined district one in the Relief Society room (the other one met in the Primary room), along with the Martins. We met six new-to-us sisters, two of whom are from Centerville and knew many of the places Sara remembers from her childhood. The meeting was good, but we had to leave a few minutes early. Helaman and Alyssa went with Sara to a meal in the PCC, while Bruce drove on via McDonald's to meet with Jung and his team. He walked the length of the campus four times, first guided by Jung, and later to go meet with some of the professors. He worked with the students to come up with an idea to resolve the issue, and is confident that they will be able to do it. There are no opportunities to teach in the Fall semester. He walked back to where the car was parked and drove over to the visitor center to pick up Sara. Together they explored a bit for food, settling in the end for Keneke's in Punaluu — from force of habit as much as anything. Home for a nap before the monthly fireside (via zoom) at 7pm. Also worked on the weekly puzzles, in which Sara finished the sudoku with blazing speed. Still both working on crossword. It feels like it was a very long day.

Wednesday morning we woke up. Sara took a call from Eli, and a couple from Jessica. After breakfast, we rested a bit, worked on puzzles, and Bruce paid this month's tithing. Sara won the crossword puzzle. Bruce spoke with Elder Seaman, who is the BYUH coordinator, who will speak with the mission leaders about him contributing there. In the evening, President Hicken called and talked with us about our assignment. He is okay with Bruce driving up to work at BYUH a couple of days a week if it is during daytime hours.

Thursday morning we are preparing to attend our first weekly zoom call in the Hawaii Laie Mission, which is called The Iron Rod (it was also the first for the mission as we learned). It is stacking up to feel more temporal than spiritual, with all of the office couples on the agenda. In the end it was just a couple of them, mostly about health issues, and it was an uplifting meeting. Then we drove up to the buffet at the PCC. Backed up traffic made us almost late, but we got our tickets and got in before closing time. Great food and friendly staff. Then, Bruce met with a couple of students in the BYUH systems department, and exchanged contact information with the lead student, Jaime Acuña (from Bohol, Philippines). We drove home and are resting now. Together, we went to the family search shift. Ordered tacos and picked them up on the way home. Raining so hard the wipers are on full. We are watching the end of Doctor Zhivago (which we started yesterday), during which Sara fell asleep; Bruce stayed up reading for a while. Another long day, what with all the driving.

Friday morning, Eli called Sara. Urk! A large cockroach is sharing our apartment with us. We spread out a little diatomaceous earth and are hoping for the best. Sara replied to a backlog of email messages, and we noted that our missionary portal now has us in the correct mission. Bruce noted that the mission map is still not updated to include the two new zones. We went shopping at Safeway, then napped, and Bruce read while Helaman and Sara made another pass at Safeway. We now have lots of fruit and ice cream.

Saturday dawned bright and sunny; the last few days have been rainy and overcast. There were some very early (before 4am) text messages that made Sara's phone chirp, but we both managed to get back to sleep. We spent the day resting. Met the elders from Waikane at Panda Express and bought them dinner and shared messages. We shopped at Safeway for eggs and Pringles, met the Sumsions from Laie (with PCC duties), and then went home to publish this blog post and go to sleep.

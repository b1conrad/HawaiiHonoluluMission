Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 46

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (May 3, 2026) we awoke to a nice sunny day, and began to get ready to return to Kaneohe, from the compact apartment we enjoyed for three days and nights.

Clockwise from top center: the room from entryway, part of tower, inside double doors,  selfie

We arrived home in time for Sara to bathe and wash her hair, and for Bruce to change into his suit, and still arrive at church in time for fast and testimony meeting, followed by a sunday school lesson on the Israelite tabernacle. Then home for lunch on various leftovers. We fell asleep after the movie The Father, which was a chilling depiction of how dementia might be, and began watching The Last Legion, which really put us to sleep (well, we fell asleep before it finished anyway)..

Monday norming, Bruce read on the front porch. We dressed in haste and met Elder Chun at the church to rehearse the song Choose You This Day, which Sara wants to invite Peter, a non-member we met at Safeway, to sing in a duet with Elder Chun. Then we came home to shower and dress for district council, where we met the new missionaries in our district and were edified by the lesson from Elder Walker, who left us with the motto, "Make your efforts match your worth." After naps and lunch, we finished the movie from yesterday.

Tuesday morning, we got ready to go to the temple — today is our district's once-per-transfer temple session. Before we left, we printed out some family names, sprayed our air conditioner with bleach and took out the filters for cleaning upon our return. The temple session was well-attended, and we got to sit side-by-side in the last room and held hands, which was delightful. We tried for a sealing session after, but there wasn't enough time.

Elders Burton, Gilmore, Maguire, Frogley, Walker, and Gibson; Sara and Bruce

On the way home, we stopped in Puna'luu and ate at Kenekes where

two hopeful dogs shared the table with us (until we began to eat anyway).

At home, putting the air conditioners back together and resting before the next bit of business. We got ready and went to an important consultation in Honolulu with Helaman. On the way home, Bruce dropped them off at Whole Foods, where they selected food while he picked out some books at the Kailua library. Bruce drove a copy of the music to Elder Chun and came back to find the weekly puzzles in the mailbox. But now the printer is offline. Reconnecting it to the router is impossibly complicated, so we'll just use it through the USB cable from now on. Seriously, HP, can't you design something better? Printed out the puzzles. Sara finished sudoku first, as per usual, and gave up on the crossword first, preferring to take a bath. Today has felt like a long day. We fell asleep partway through the movie The Portrait.

Wednesday morning a couple of nice guys came and cleaned out our dryer vent (this is a once-every-three-year maintenance thing done in Haiku Point (no charge)). We both finished the crossword puzzle with a tied score. Thinking about what we will do after the mission, and looking into possibilities at BYU Hawaii. We went grocery shopping at Safeway, and asked Peter if he would be willing to come and sing with us at church; he declined, but sang the song again for Sara. We came home and ate; Sara took a bath, while Bruce worked on this document and his picos, as he does every night at 8pm and then again at 9:10pm.

Thursday, we enjoyed the morning; Sara got up to bathe, Bruce showered, and we dressed and washed up some dishes so that we could make breakfast. A game of Skip Bo ensued, and then Bruce prepared our monthly check and mailed it. Next was the mission-wide weekly SYS meeting over zoom. Sisters came for Books of Mormon.

Sara organized a selfie with Sisters NaoPhiankohm and Arancon; smiles all around

In the evening, we drove to the stake center to staff the family history library.

Friday we got up a little earlier than usual, prepared, ate breakfast so that we can leave at 8:45am to attend the next zone conference in the tabernacle in Honolulu. Before picture:

The meeting itself was very good, and we were uplifted. We ate at Safeway on Kapahulu on the way home, then napped until 4:30pm when Elder Chun picked us up and drove to the buffet in Waikiki, which was great. While there, we met a recently returned missionary who had spent some time in Bruce's home town of Taber Alberta Canada, and who knows our cousins John and Ellen (whom we visited last year, as recorded in our week 0 blog post). Sleep will come sometime after 9:30 tonight. Another long, and eventful, day.

Saturday we went to the mother's day breakfast, where Bruce helped with the preparation and serving, and Sara ate with the sisters. Laundry day. Two elders came to pick up ten copies of the Book of Mormon.

The talk went well; Sara claimed that there were a few minutes of complete silence after Bruce's talk before the intermediate hymn got started. The concluding speaker said she had been asked at 8am the same morning; her talk was good too. Sunday School was a lesson about Moses as well, and shoehorned well with Bruce's talk. We came home and had lunch and naps.

Monday dawned overcast. Kyle came earlier than planned and inspected the apartment, on behalf of its owners. We're hoping they will authorize a new range and oven, because this one turns itself off sometimes, and other times spontaneously locks the control panel.

Tuesday we attended the district council meeting, bringing a case of Books of Mormon. The training was on consistent consecration. Bruce continued to rest, keeping his shingles episode happy. We finished both puzzles within seconds, so a tie.

Wednesday, we are preparing for a possible visit from our next mission president, who we are told will be visiting all the apartments in our zone today. Oh, this excludes senior couple apartments. Bruce remembered and located again a piece he wrote some time ago about Moses, Commandments without confabulation, and inserted a link to it into this document

Thursday we watched the SYS zoom meeting. Sara worked on some deviled eggs for Friday. Bruce is still resting and recuperating. Sara drove the truck to Honolulu and delivered a bed (being cleared out by a senior couple leaving) to Danny. That made him very happy.

Even though he is only 13 years old, he is well over six feet tall and three hundred pounds and had been dealing with a piece of foam on a twin frame. This might look like a twin, but it is a queen, a much better fit for him. We have dibs on the same couple's king size bed; we'll see. In the evening we did a shift at the family search center. Home tired. Oh, earlier, Bruce had gone over to the stake center knowing our mission leaders would be on break between interviews to say goodbye to them and let them know we admire them. We also heard from Sister Haycock.

Friday morning came. Sara tried the Safeway shopping app, and although it was a bit frustrating at times, liked the overall result: quite a bit of food with a large amount of savings. We are now getting ready to go to the monthly Oahu seniors dinner, the last one we will get to attend since we will be in the other mission. Good food, company, and fun.

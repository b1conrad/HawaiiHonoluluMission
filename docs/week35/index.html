Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 35

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (February 15, 2026) morning was somewhat eventful; Bruce, while out on the porch around sunrise spotted a pair of doves close enough to touch on the railing, and then heard cooing sounds above his head. There is a nest above the light at our front door.

Then, after Sara spoke on the phone with our friends from Orangeville, the Slanes, about Tiffany, Ammon called to share the news that he and Elizabeth are expecting a child. Other than that, breakfast and getting ready to go to church as usual.

The meetings were good, including the Sunday School lesson by Scott, with whom we spoke briefly outside afterwards. On the way home we stopped at an open house for a nearby apartment that is for sale for 665K and is smaller than ours. We talked and napped and ate, and did one load of laundry, to finish what we had started yesterday.

Monday, we decided to prepare for the next ten days; we have a number of things to do, including a couple of things with Bruce's cousins, and a week with our friends who are coming on Ash Wednesday. Eli called at 7am for some help with the visitation since today is a holiday. Bruce spent quite a bit of time finding out how to get discount tickets for PCC. We have to get their website working for us. Barring that — which … we have to wait for tomorrow because helpers are not working on this national holiday — we would need to make reservations in person for later on the same day. We may try this on Friday. Eating, reading, napping, thinking about the coming days and sharing plans with Sara's friend, Sarah.

Tuesday morning, Sara got up first, then Bruce arose at 7am to get ready for a meeting with Phil's students, before which he did some maintenance on his pico engine, including renewing the certificate. Bruce enjoyed the meeting and got a chance to mention his prior work on PicoStack.org which was fun. Next we will be preparing to attend the district council meeting at 10am. Bruce was able to make a couple of phone calls; he got his password reset for PCC discount tickets, and arranged for Sara's transcript to be made available to BYU Pathways.

From: Sister Pugh, envelope (not to scale), and Sister Haycock, now companions on Kauai.

The lesson at district council was given by new-to-us Elders Miller and Walker (no relation to folks from Taber and Raymond, respectively), about their take on the plan of salvation, spelling out the letters HA (breath of life) in a unique way. Sara assisted Elder Fehoko, using Bruce's phone, in signing up for a dental appointment online. Bruce also drove to sell some empties, deposit some cash in the bank, and return a package in the mail, returning to find a letter for Elder Quiner, a package for Sister Krupp, and a delightful pair of notes to us from two sisters who were recently in our zone as part of the two sister companionships we used to have and now find themselves together on another island. We also had phone calls from Jessica, who is having problems at work, and some talks with Helaman.

Wednesday we began packing our suitcases; we are going to spend a week in Waikiki, in the room next to our friends the Spencers from Saint George. Much laundry was done, and we left shortly after noon, passing through Kailua so that Bruce could pick up a requested book at the library while Sara purchased some water bottles and some hot food at Whole Foods. We were just driving past Denise's place and stopped to give her a ride to work, and got to the hotel around 2:30, unpacked, and then drove to pick up our friends where they had parked their car and we all went to the Chart House, with the idea that we would listen to some entertainment from Kamuela (in our ward bishopric) there. But Bruce got the timing wrong. We went in right when the happy hour started, at 3:30pm, and ate and talked, but we were done before 5pm and he was just arriving to set up for his performance, and we were all ready for sleep (especially those newly arrived after a long day of travel from a place three hours later).

Reece and Kerry, Sara, Kent and Sarah, Bruce; Spencers again; Bruce and Kerry, the cousins

We drove back to the hotel, well actually, to a parking place quite a distance away, and walked to the hotel. When we got there, Bruce realized we had forgotten a very important small bag, and he had to walk all the way back to the car to get it. Watching a movie. We slept on and off, with quite a bit of noise outside and the wind howling (like it used to in Canada in a blizzard).

Thursday morning, Sara got up early to go for a walk with both Kent and Sarah, the latter of whom got her feet wet in the ocean. Sara fixed breakfast of chicken purchased yesterday and bread with no butter since Bruce was the one who packed the fridge bag. Preparing to go to the zone conference in Mililani. We left just before 8am to walk to where the car was parked beyond the zoo, and drove off, deciding not to go directly onto the H1, but instead turned off onto King Street which became Berentania, and took Punchbowl onto the freeway. We didn't take the H2 either, but took highway 99 into Mililani, stopping at Safeway to purchase lunch for Sara and some hardboiled eggs. The meeting itself was quite moving, with our president relating to us apostolic blessings quoted by Elder Ransband from D&C 109:71. Group picture on next page. After the meal (of orange chicken, rice, and salad), our president interviewed us for temple recommends. Then we left and drove to Costco for a little shopping and gas, and left there at 3:26pm, dropping Sara and our acquisitions at the hotel and then going to find a parking spot. Bruce walked back from (the very same) parking spot, getting in at 4:59pm. After resting, at 6pm, Bruce walked to the beach for sunset; it was high tide and very cloudy. He turned around and walked back to write this entry. On the way, he stopped at ABC to price water, with the intention of returning with money to buy some, but Sara said nevermind.

Front couples: Peterson, Thomas, Bennett, Andersen, Chun, Larson, Conrad

Friday we have an appointment to do sealings in the temple. We got up and left just before 7am, walking to our cars, and they followed us to our parking lot in Kaneohe, where we parked the Scion in a visitor spot and then Kent drove up to Laie with us in the back seat. We arrived twenty minutes early, and then had an enjoyable session in sealing room 4 (on the third floor) followed by a few minutes in the celestial room. Then, Elder Mangum took pictures of us.

We and the Spencers facing the temple doors, then turning in-place in front of said doors.

We drove to the temple beach parking lot and walked into view of the ocean. Kent and Bruce waited while Sarah walked down to the water for just a few minutes. When we arrived at the PCC, we found it closed for an hour, so we drove on to Keneke and ate outside in back.

Goats eating on a platform, watched by a chicken; turning right: Kent walking and Sara at table.

When we arrived in Kaneohe, to our parking lot, it was raining, so we waited a couple of minutes and then as the rain started to subside, Bruce walked up to our porch and brought down our two bodyboards and put them in their trunk; they drove off and Sara and Bruce drove to Foodland to buy some things for Helaman, which we dropped off and then Bruce drove us to Waikiki. We carried our stuff upstairs and then Bruce drove off to find a parking spot (closer this time) and walked back. Some reading, naps, and phone calls ensued. Slept well.

Saturday, Bruce awakened at 5:36am and dozed quietly until Sara awoke about 7. She fixed a wonderful breakfast in the tiny kitchenette, and Sarah asked if we could walk to the beach, which we did in a drizzle. Sara and Bruce went all the way into the water and had a good time.

Sara and Bruce getting wet in the ocean; Bruce and Sara sheltering from the heavy rain later.

We met a couple of Ukrainians who live in Sacramento (where he runs GD Smart Builders) and had a fun time talking with them. Sarah walked back to the hotel, while we rinsed off our feet and beach shoes, then spoke for a while with a nice family (of three) from Vermont. By the time we started back to the hotel the rain was just pouring down, so we arrived soaked; showered, then rested. Meanwhile, Kent had been attending his sister's funeral remotely. Later, all four of us walked to their car in just pouring rain, pausing under a shelter near the zoo until it calmed down a bit. We drove to Whole Foods and bought lunch, which we ate there, and then put our leftover food and six gallons of water into the trunk, and went down the elevator and walked to the beach. Sara sat on a bench part way there, and the rest of us walked back to get the car and pick her up. We got out of the car with the water and food, taking them upstairs, while they went to find a parking space and then walked back.

The remainder of the day was spent resting. Bruce has come down with a cold, which is sad. The elders called asking to borrow a blow dryer for their shoes, so we arranged to have it set out on our porch for them to pick up after 8:30pm.

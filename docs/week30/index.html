Hawaii Honolulu Mission — Week 30

Bruce and Sara Conrad, 2025–26

Sunday (January 11, 2026) we spent some time studying Come Follow Me and are almost caught up. We opened our fast, asking for the powers that be to declare the Finance 300 class to be suitable for Sara's BGS degree program. We attended church meetings in our ward, and came home and made lunch, then tried to nap. We attended the stake missionary fireside at 6pm, which was very nicely done.

Monday breakfast reminded me of a stick figure person. Anthropomorphism: which is a thing people do, seeing objects in clouds, and in star fields; people in food on a plate; or even a face in a couple of drops of water on the edge of the bathtub.

After breakfast, we drove to locate the mechanic whom Nadine recommended for our truck safety check, and then on to fill the gas tank at the Costco in Waipio, followed by grocery shopping there. Another errand in the late afternoon, in which we picked up a prescription and sold 21 empty plastic bottles. It took quite a while to get all of the food into the fridge and freezer.

Tuesday we are getting ready for the district council meeting. After the meeting, we looked at all of the cars and recommended cleaning that should be done before the interviews on Thursday. Bruce felt discouraged and wanted to go home, but there isn't any home to go to and we just have to tough it out. We went shopping for a few things and came home (i.e. the apartment) for lunch (Sara heated some delicious pot stickers with salad and fruit) and naps. We learned a lot in the study group that our ward held at 6:30pm. It will move to Thursday for the fourth week.

Wednesday will be a busy day preparing food for the luncheon with the mission leaders between interviews tomorrow. Also printed out some music for Sister Paez and Sorensen, and set up a car wash station for Sisters Krupp and Boehme (of Magrath, Alberta). We took the watermelon to the chapel and we cut it up in the kitchen there (by we, we mean Sara) and left it in the fridge along with some sparkling water to chill overnight. Came home after dark.

Thursday was a busy day preparing food and delivering it to the stake center and setting it up on the serving table, etc. Followed by cleanup. We both napped and then got up to go to a baptism service for Seth, at 6pm.

We left before pictures. Seth is kneeling front and center (with leis). Next to him: Sister and Elder Larson; Sisters Boehme and Krupp, who taught him.

Friday, we got up early and Bruce spoke with Matt Greene (of BYU) about Sara's last class, and got some bad news (re finance), but had a good talk. We went to another baptism at 7pm.

Sister Paez, Aaron, (baptized by) Sam, Sister Sorensen. Leis always adorn the new member.

Saturday, preparing to participate in a going-away party for Moheni Matavale, hosted by DLB. Bruce drove a short errand to sell back empty plastic and aluminum, by count. Photo of party getting underway, just before Elder Chun (also in white shirt) gave the blessing on the food.
